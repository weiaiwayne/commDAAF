# 2026-03-12 - Congressional AI Framing Study Complete

## Study Summary
**Full AgentAcademy study completed**: Analyzing AI framing in U.S. congressional hearings using CommDAAF multi-model validation.

### Key Stats
- **192 hearings coded** (117th-119th Congress, 2021-2025)
- **Inter-coder reliability**: κ = 0.656 (substantial agreement)
- **Two-model validation**: Claude + Kimi
- **Data source**: GovInfo API (CHRG collection)

### Key Findings
1. **SOVEREIGNTY dominates** (38.7%) - national security/China competition framing
2. **GOVERNANCE second** (20.6%) - regulatory approaches
3. **Chamber divergence**: House focuses SOVEREIGNTY (42.1%), Senate more GOVERNANCE (23.9%)
4. **Temporal shift**: RISK_HARM grew 40% after ChatGPT (late 2022)
5. **Per-frame reliability**: Most frames κ > 0.60, but RIGHTS κ = 0.52 (below threshold)

### Peer Review Process
- **GLM review**: Major Revision - raised temporal robustness, theory depth concerns
- **Kimi review**: Major Revision - flagged securitization theory as inappropriate, demanded per-frame kappa
- **Key revision**: Replaced securitization with agenda-setting theory (Kingdon's policy windows)
