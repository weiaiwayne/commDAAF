## Recent Studies (2020-2025)

### 1. Clinton, J.D. & Huang, T.F. (2025)
**"Prediction Markets? The Accuracy and Efficiency of $2.4 Billion in the 2024 Presidential Election"**
- **Source:** OSF Preprint
- **URL:** https://osf.io/download/d5yx2/
- **Key Finding:** Largest study of Polymarket data ($2.4B traded). Markets arrive at correct final forecasts despite negative serial correlation in prices.
- **Cited by:** 1

### 2. Gruca, T.S. & Rietz, T.A. (2025)
**"Iowa Electronic Markets: Forecasting the 2024 US Presidential Election"**
- **Source:** *PS: Political Science & Politics* (Cambridge)
- **URL:** https://doi.org/10.1017/S1049096524000921
- **Key Finding:** IEM vote-share and winner-takes-all markets for 2024. Continues 36-year track record.
- **Cited by:** 4

### 3. Sethi, R., Seager, J., Morstatter, F., & Benjamin, D. (2025)
**"Political Prediction and the Wisdom of Crowds"**
- **Source:** *ACM Proceedings*
- **URL:** https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3715928.3737483
- **Key Finding:** Compares simultaneously operating online prediction markets. Discusses probabilistic election forecasting methods.
- **Cited by:** 1

### 4. Cordoba Otalora, F. & Themistocleous, M. (2025)
**"Beyond the Polls: Quantifying Early Signals in Decentralized Prediction Markets with Cross-Correlation and Dynamic Time Warping"**
- **Source:** *Future Internet* (MDPI)
- **URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/1999-5903/17/11/487
- **Key Finding:** Analyzes divergence between prediction market forecasts in 2024. Uses cross-correlation methods.
- **Open Access:** Yes

### 5. Saiegh, S. (2024)
**"Forecasting the 2024 US Presidential Election: Financial Markets Trump Opinion Polls and Prediction Markets"**
- **Source:** UCSD Working Paper
- **URL:** https://socialsciences.ucsd.edu/~ssaiegh/forecast_2024.pdf
- **Key Finding:** Financial market signals outperform both polls and prediction markets. Polls underestimated Trump; markets overestimated.

### 6. Crane, H. & Vinson, D. (2023)
**"Models vs. Markets: Forecasting the 2020 US Election"**
- **Source:** *The Journal of Prediction Markets*
- **URL:** https://www.ubplj.org/index.php/jpm/article/view/2068
- **Key Finding:** Compares statistical modeling vs. prediction market approaches for 2020 election.
- **Cited by:** 3

### 7. Sethi, R., Seager, J., Cai, E., & Benjamin, D.M. (2021)
**"Models, Markets, and the Forecasting of Elections"**
- **Source:** arXiv
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.04936
- **Key Finding:** Proposes bringing models into direct interaction with humans in prediction markets via virtual traders.
- **Cited by:** 3

### 8. Leister, H.L. (2021)
**"Prediction Markets vs. Political Polls: Forecasting Election Outcomes"**
- **Source:** University of Arizona Thesis
- **URL:** https://repository.arizona.edu/handle/10150/666656
- **Key Finding:** Systematic comparison of prediction market vs. polling accuracy.

### 9. McGee, Z.A. & Hall, P.D. (2025)
**"Using Prediction Markets as a Tool for Classroom and Civic Engagement"**
- **Source:** *Journal of Political Science Education* (Taylor & Francis)
- **URL:** https://doi.org/10.1080/15512169.2024.2385366
- **Key Finding:** Demonstrates prediction markets as educational and civic engagement tools.
- **Cited by:** 3

### 10. Travin, A. (2025)
**"Forecasting the Future: The Argument for Prediction Markets in the Intelligence Community"**
- **Source:** SSRN
- **URL:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5864862
- **Key Finding:** Argues for prediction markets in government intelligence applications.

## Classic/Foundational Studies (Pre-2020)

### 11. Berg, J.E., Nelson, F.D., & Rietz, T.A. (2008)
**"Prediction Market Accuracy in the Long Run"**
- **Source:** *International Journal of Forecasting*, 24(2), 285-300
- **URL:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijforecast.2008.03.007
- **Key Finding:** IEM beat polls 74% of the time across 5 presidential elections (1988-2004). **Gold standard citation.**
- **Cited by:** 500+

### 12. Wolfers, J. & Zitzewitz, E. (2006)
**"Prediction Markets in Theory and Practice"**
- **Source:** NBER Working Paper 12083
- **URL:** https://www.nber.org/papers/w12083
- **Key Finding:** Foundational theoretical framework. Market prices = probability estimates.
- **Published in:** *New Palgrave Dictionary of Economics* (2008)
- **Cited by:** 1000+

### 13. Wolfers, J. & Zitzewitz, E. (2004)
**"Prediction Markets"**
- **Source:** *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, 18(2), 107-126
- **URL:** https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/0895330041371321
- **Key Finding:** Derives theoretical basis from efficient markets hypothesis.
- **Cited by:** 2000+

### 14. Rothschild, D. & Wolfers, J. (2014)
**"Forecasting Elections: Comparing Prediction Markets, Polls, and Their Biases"**
- **Source:** *Public Opinion Quarterly*, 78(1), 33-54
- **Key Finding:** Markets have smaller mean absolute error than polls; correct faster for biases.

## LLM/Multi-Agent Studies (Relevant to VibePolitics)

### 15. Zhang, X. et al. (2024)
**"ElectionSim: Massive Population Election Simulation Powered by Large Language Model Driven Agents"**
- **Source:** arXiv:2410.20746
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.20746
- **Key Finding:** Simulated elections with 14,490 LLM agents. Validated against real outcomes.

### 16. Timoneda, J.C. & Wibbels, E. (2022)
**"Spikes and Variance: Using Google Trends to Detect and Forecast Protests"**
- **Source:** *Political Analysis*, 30(1), 1-18
- **URL:** https://doi.org/10.1017/pan.2020.18
- **Key Finding:** Novel variance-in-time method for Google Trends analysis.

### 17. Behnert, J., Lajic, D., & Bauer, P.C. (2024)
**"Can We Predict Multi-party Elections with Google Trends Data?"**
- **Source:** *Journal of Big Data*, 11(1)
- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40537-023-00868-4
- **Key Finding:** GT adds predictive value beyond polls alone; best when combined.

## Key Themes

1. **Markets beat polls** — Consistent finding since Berg et al. (2008)
2. **$2.4B in 2024** — Largest prediction market election ever (Polymarket)
3. **IEM still running** — 36-year track record, gold standard data
4. **Fusion approaches** — Best results when combining markets + polls + other signals
5. **LLM agents emerging** — ElectionSim shows feasibility of agent-based forecasting
