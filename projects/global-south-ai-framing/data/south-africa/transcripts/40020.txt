Meeting Summary
Video
The Standing Committee on Appropriations
heard
public submissions on the 2024 Medium Term Budget Policy Statement (MTBPS), the Adjustments Appropriation Bill, and the Special Appropriation Bill.
The meeting featured submissions from several stakeholders.
The
United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund (Unicef) focused on the implications of the 2024 MTBPS for children's rights, highlighting systemic inequities in education, health, and social services exacerbated by stagnant spending and social sector cuts.
It
emphasised the need for targeted investments in frontline services and infrastructure to ensure equity and long-term social development.
The South African Human Rights Commission raised concerns about the impact of budget reductions on its ability to fulfil its constitutional mandate. It warned that financial constraints were undermining its work on systemic issues such as housing, water, sanitation, and the protection of vulnerable groups. The Commission called for greater funding to strengthen its oversight role and ensure human rights violations are effectively addressed.
The National Khoi-San Council
highlighted the systemic
marginalisation
and lack of constitutional recognition for indigenous peoples. She detailed the absence of targeted budget allocations, inadequate support for cultural preservation, and ongoing challenges in accessing basic services.
The Council
called for the establishment of mechanisms to ensure fair representation and resource allocation for Khoi and San communities.
The Congress of South African Trade Unions criticised
government's fiscal consolidation approach,
emphasising
its detrimental effects on public services, job creation, and economic growth.
It
advocated for a stimulus-driven fiscal policy, arguing for increased investment in state-owned enterprises, social relief programs, and employment initiatives to address unemployment and inequality.
Youth Capital
emphasised
youth unemployment and public employment programmes, Injini call
ed
for
prioritising
educational technology in schools, and Masincedane United Residents Association
made a
plea for improved housing and basic services for informal settlement residents.
Each organisation presented actionable recommendations to address systemic challenges, urging the
C
ommittee to ensure budget allocations reflect

the needs of
marginalised
communities.
The
C
ommittee engaged actively with the presenters, with
M
embers posing detailed questions about specific proposals.
They
raised concerns about corruption, inefficiencies in public service delivery, and systemic inequities. There were pointed discussions about the representation of Khoi and San interests, the effectiveness of public employment programmes, and the role of government in ensuring equitable access to resources.
The
Committee announced that it would deliberate on the hearings the next Tuesday and its
final report
wa
s expected to be tabled in Parliament on 5 December 2024.

United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund (
UNICEF
) submission
The submission was delivered by Mr
Russell Wildeman, Social Policy Manager, UNICEF South Africa.
He began with

of children in South Africa
(slide 3)

He then presented some of the foundational policy and budgetary tools to support children in South Africa (slides 4-6). Of concern were a drop in the diphtheria, tetanus and pertussis (DTP) vaccination rate, the high level of severe child food poverty (one in four children), and the high number of children living with untreated HIV. The submission analysed the MTBPS through the lenses of adequacy, equity, and efficiency and effectiveness (slides 7-12). Finally, four recommendations were made (slides 13-17):
- The proposed increases to government’s infrastructure spending are welcomed. However, it is vital that both economic and social infrastructure receive the necessary attention.
- National Treasury should pioneer the introduction of explicit budget lines in social development budgets that identify prevention and early intervention expenditures.
- While UNICEF understands government’s focus on the wage bill, frontline services and personnel must be protected against capricious budget cuts.
- Government should pay meticulous attention to system-level equity via the provincial and local government equitable share formulas, and the different manifestations of equity for children.
