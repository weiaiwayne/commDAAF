2025/26 Annual Performance Plans
The Select Committee on Social Services met to receive a briefing from the Department of Women, Youth and Persons with Disabilities on its revised Strategic Plan for 2025–2030, Annual Performance Plan (APP) for 2025/26, and related budget allocations. The briefing emphasised the Department’s coordinating and advocacy mandate, with specific focus on mainstreaming the rights of women, youth, and persons with disabilities across all spheres of government and sectors of society. It was noted that these plans were aligned with the National Development Plan (NDP) and the Medium Term Development Plan (MTDP).
The Committee was briefed on forthcoming legislative and regulatory instruments aimed at strengthening accountability and driving socio-economic inclusion. These included draft bills on gender equality, youth development, and the rights of persons with disabilities, as well as a gender mainstreaming regulatory framework. Progress on interventions to address gender-based violence and femicide was also outlined, including the forthcoming operationalisation of an independent national council and associated monitoring systems.
The Department reported a total budget of R1.361 billion for 2025/26, with the majority earmarked for transfers to its public entities. After fixed costs and ringfenced allocations, limited funding remained available for direct programme implementation. The Committee noted this constraint.
Members raised concerns regarding slow progress in the mainstreaming of disability across government, continued non-compliance with existing frameworks, and the lack of enforceable accountability mechanisms. Questions were posed about what was being done differently to ensure the realisation of the Department’s mandate. Concerns were also raised about reports of inappropriate conduct during recruitment processes within the public sector and whether internal safeguards were in place.
The Department responded that its role was primarily facilitative and that it did not possess enforcement powers to compel compliance by other departments. It confirmed that the matter of disability employment had been escalated to Cabinet and that incremental targets had been approved. However, resistance from other departments remained a significant challenge. The Committee was encouraged to continue exercising its oversight role to hold the wider public service accountable.
The Committee agreed that an outstanding question would be submitted in writing and confirmed that any further requests for clarification would be sent to the Department.
Meeting report
The Chairperson welcomed all present, including the Ministry and further reminded the Committee that it had already received a briefing from the Department of Women, Youth and Persons with Disabilities (DWYPD) in April 2025 regarding this matter. She therefore requested that the Department provide a brief overview before moving directly to the revisions made since the earlier presentation.
Minister’s opening remarks
Ms Sindisiwe Chikunga, Minister of Women, Youth and Persons with Disabilities, confirmed that she would need to leave the meeting early due to a scheduled sitting in the National Assembly, which included voting on the budget. She requested that she be permitted to depart following her opening remarks and noted that the Department’s delegation, led by the Director-General, would remain behind to respond to any questions or comments from Members.
She confirmed that the Department was honoured to present both the Annual Performance Plan (APP) for 2025/26 and the Strategic Plan for 2025–2030. The presentation was framed by the Department’s constitutional mandate to coordinate, advocate, mainstream, monitor, evaluate, and provide strategic leadership toward the socio-economic empowerment of women, youth, and persons with disabilities—groups that represent the overwhelming majority of the marginalised in South Africa.
Minister Chikunga drew inspiration from the 70th anniversary of the Freedom Charter and the Bill of Rights, noting that transforming, empowering, and transitioning marginalised groups was not merely a policy objective but a constitutional imperative. She stressed that the APP and Strategic Plan were aligned with the Medium Term Development Plan (MTDP) and the National Development Plan (NDP), and they articulated the Department’s projected contribution to the three overarching strategic priorities of the seventh administration: inclusive economic growth and job creation; reducing poverty and the cost of living; and building a capable, ethical, and developmental state.
Socio-Economic Context and Mandate Realisation
Minister Chikunga acknowledged the progress made over the past three decades but noted that the lived experiences of women, youth, and persons with disabilities remained far from ideal. Women, in particular, continued to be the face of poverty and unemployment, were disproportionately confined to informal or undervalued work, and remained vulnerable to gender-based violence and femicide (GBVF). She noted that even decades after the Beijing Declaration, gender disparities persisted across national, regional, and global contexts.
Minister Chikunga argued that inclusive economic growth was not possible while women remained economically marginalised. She also stressed that the mere existence of progressive legislation did not translate into implementation or lived realities. The next five years would therefore focus on cultivating enforcement mechanisms to strengthen monitoring, evaluation, and accountability.
Strategic Priorities
Minister Chikunga outlined the five strategic priorities set out in the APP and Strategic Plan:
Accelerating the realisation of rights for targeted groups through legislative frameworks, advocacy, mainstreaming, and programming.
Leveraging existing laws and master plans to institutionalise socio-economic empowerment as a measurable target across all spheres of government and the private sector.
Strengthening research, coordination, monitoring, and evaluation to support evidence-based programming and shift the Department’s Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) from input to output-based measures. She stated that the Department would work with higher education institutions, research organisations, and the Council for Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR)
Enhancing technological capability through internal automation and digital inclusion for vulnerable groups in rural and urban areas.
Modelling, coordinating, and mainstreaming catalytic projects, including emerging industrial pathways, apprenticeships, and empowerment programmes.
Legislative and Regulatory Instruments
Minister Chikunga highlighted a set of core legislative and regulatory instruments planned for tabling during the seventh administration, including:
The Promotion of Women’s Rights, Empowerment, and Gender Equality (WEGE) Bill, currently under refinement and intended to give effect to section 9 of the Constitution.
The South African Youth Development Bill, which is at draft stage and intended to strengthen youth development accountability.
The Promotion and Protection of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Bill, being developed in partnership with the South African Law Reform Commission to build a disability-responsive state.
Regulations under the Public Procurement Act, developed in collaboration with National Treasury to enable gender-responsive value chains.
A Socio-Economic Empowerment Index, which would be used to monitor participation and address structural barriers to women’s economic empowerment.
The finalisation of a Gender Mainstreaming Regulatory Framework, which would standardise the integration of gender considerations in departmental budgeting, procurement, employment, and service delivery.
Minister Chikunga emphasised that youth unemployment, particularly among young women, remained a critical barrier to national development. The Department would conduct a midterm evaluation of the National Youth Policy (2020–2030) to assess policy relevance and adjust instruments to better serve marginalised youth.
Flagship Priorities and GBVF Interventions
Minister Chikunga reported that the National Council on Gender-Based Violence and Femicide Act had been passed and that the process to establish the Council was underway. Once operational, it would be South Africa's first independent statutory body responsible for the national response to GBVF. The National Assembly was currently finalising the appointment process for civil society and private sector representatives to the Council.
She added that President Ramaphosa had established an Inter-Ministerial Committee on GBVF, alcohol, and substance abuse, tasked with expediting intervention strategies. The Department had launched a national GBVF dashboard and was finalising a prevention strategy, monitoring and evaluation framework. The work of the G20 Working Group on Women's Empowerment, chaired by South Africa, would also continue to focus on the care economy, financial inclusion, and combating GBVF.
Youth Empowerment through Innovation and Industry
Minister Chikunga recalled that in the 2023/24 financial year, the Department had committed to mobilising government and society to support youth development. This commitment had been honoured through the conceptualisation and rollout of the South African National Service Institute, launched by the Deputy President, Mr Paul Mashatile, in May 2024. The initiative aimed to prepare youth to become market-oriented smallholder farmers, Artificial Intelligence (AI) and robotics instructors, cybersecurity entrepreneurs, and industrial drone pilots, among other professions.
The Department had submitted a comprehensive funding proposal to the National Skills Fund and was also engaging other public and private sector funders. She acknowledged the support of the CSIR and other stakeholders who had contributed technical expertise to the project.
National Youth Development Agency (NYDA)
Minister Chikunga informed the Committee that a new CEO had been appointed to the NYDA and would commence duties on 1 July 2025. A new Board had also been appointed and would begin on 1 August 2025. The Department intended to finalise a shareholder compact with the Board before the end of the financial year and would introduce Annual General Meetings in September, in line with governance standards for Schedule 3A entities. She stressed that the NYDA must become a truly representative agency for young people across all demographics.
Minister Chikunga concluded her remarks by thanking the Committee and requesting permission to leave the meeting. She handed over to the Director-General (DG) to present the Department’s Strategic Plan and Annual Performance Plan.
Briefing by the Department of Women, Youth and Persons with Disabilities on the Strategic Plan, 2025/26 Annual Performance Plan and Budget Vote
Adv Mikateko Maluleke, DG, DWYPD, noted that the Minister’s opening remarks had already provided a comprehensive summary of the Department’s strategic priorities and focus areas. She therefore proposed that the Committee proceed directly to the financial component of the presentation. She then handed over to the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) to present on the Department’s budget and financial allocations for the 2025/26 financial year and over the medium term.
Ms Desree Legwale, CFO, DWYPD, stated that the Department’s total allocation for the 2025/26 financial year stood at R1.361 billion. This allocation would decline over the outer years of the Medium Term Expenditure Framework (MTEF) period, largely due to the cessation of the Presidential Employment Initiative allocations, which were not extended beyond the 2025/26 period.
She outlined the programme-specific allocations as follows:
Programme 1: Administration was allocated R125 million.
Programme 2: Advocacy and Mainstreaming for the Rights of Women (AMRW) received R139.033 million.
Programme 3: Monitoring, Evaluation, Research and Coordination was allocated R44.464 million.
Programme 4: Rights of Persons with Disabilities received R15.3 million.
Programme 5: National Youth Development was allocated a budget under the broader transfer to entities line item.
Of the total R1.361 billion allocation, R1.122 billion had been earmarked for transfers to the Department’s two public entities—the National Youth Development Agency (NYDA) and the Commission for Gender Equality (CGE). This left the Department with a direct operational budget of approximately R239.644 million.
Ms Legwale explained that of the remaining departmental allocation:
R180 million had been set aside for compensation of employees, covering salaries and wages.
R89.6 million had been allocated to goods and services, of which:
9 million was ringfenced for mandatory contractual obligations, including leases and IT services.
2 million had been received as an additional allocation for G20-related activities.
R5 million was earmarked for the establishment of the secretariat of the National Council on Gender-Based Violence and Femicide (NCGBVF).
After all earmarked and fixed costs, only R38.7 million remained available for the implementation of the Department’s programmes and projects, as outlined in the 2025/26 Annual Performance Plan.
Ms Legwale concluded her presentation by noting the constraints this posed for programme delivery and thanked the Committee for the opportunity to present.

Discussion
Ms N du Plessis (DA, Gauteng) welcomed the emphasis on protecting vulnerable groups in society. She noted that this was a recurring concern and asked what the Department was doing differently to ensure that it fulfilled its mandate to improve the lives of women, youth, and persons with disabilities. She enquired whether there had been any response to the ongoing rumours that some officials were allegedly offering employment to young women on condition that they provide sexual favours. She asked what internal interventions were in place to address such allegations, including reports of individuals being asked to share hotel rooms in exchange for job opportunities in the public sector.
Mr M Feni (ANC, Eastern Cape) conveyed his appreciation to Minister Chikunga for availing herself, given her demanding schedule. He also expressed gratitude for the presentation, which detailed the relevant budget items. He reiterated that he did not wish to pose questions, but rather to acknowledge the work being done and the importance of capacitating the Department through the budget process. He apologised for the unstable network connection.
Responses
Adv Maluleke explained that the Department’s role was to facilitate the mainstreaming of disability across all spheres of government, and emphasised that it could not on its own empower persons with disabilities. She said that an enabling system across government was essential, and highlighted that each department – whether responsible for education, water, tourism, or other sectors – had a responsibility to mainstream disability within its mandate. She confirmed that the Department worked hard to collaborate with others, but noted that many departments were either resistant or uncooperative, particularly in relation to the employment of persons with disabilities.
She reported that the Department had escalated the matter to Cabinet and requested an increase in the employment of persons with disabilities in the public service, in line with the White Paper, which required a 7% representation by 2030. She stated that Cabinet approved incremental targets – 3% by 2024/25 and 4% by 2025/26 – but despite this, government departments remained stagnant at around 1.2%. She stressed that the Department did not have the authority to enforce compliance.
Adv Maluleke recommended that the Committee invite the Department of Public Service and Administration to explain when it would implement the Cabinet decision. She noted that the Department had developed four frameworks on reasonable accommodation and related matters, but these were not being implemented across the public service. She reiterated that the failure to mainstream disability was a systemic issue and not solely the responsibility of the Department. She then handed over to Ms Mabelebele to expand on the matter.
Ms Phuti Mabelebele, Chief Director: Advocacy and Mainstreaming Rights of People with Disabilities, elaborated on the Department’s advocacy and mainstreaming programmes. She acknowledged that the mainstreaming of disability indicators and targets was not progressing at the desired level. She said that the Department engaged with government departments and monitored implementation through its monitoring and evaluation unit. Where departments struggled, the Department provided technical assistance and engaged directly with executive authorities and accounting officers to ensure alignment with the requirements of the White Paper.
She added that while some progress had been made, the overall pace remained slow. She welcomed the oversight work of parliamentary Committees, noting that this played a significant role in strengthening accountability. She confirmed that the Department continued to work closely with organisations of persons with disabilities, as required by the White Paper. She emphasised the importance of stakeholder forums to facilitate collaboration. She concluded by stating that the Department, through the Minister, was engaging departments at both political and administrative levels to intensify accountability and improve implementation.
The Chairperson stated that Ms du Plessis would resubmit her question in writing, as it had not yet been adequately addressed. She thanked the Department for the presentation and the responses provided during the engagement. She indicated that if further clarity was required, the Committee would submit additional questions to the Director-General in writing. She then released the Director-General and her team from the meeting.
Adoption of Minutes
Minutes dated 16 July 2025
Minutes were adopted with no amendments.
Closing remarks
The Chairperson asked the Committee to proceed to the next item on the agenda. The minutes of the meeting held on 16 July 2025 were adopted. She thanked Members for their participation and extended her appreciation to the support staff. She wished everyone well for the upcoming recess period, noting that Members would continue their work in their respective constituencies.
Meeting adjourned.
