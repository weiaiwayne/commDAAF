Opening
The Speaker opened the meeting at 08:30 and welcomed everyone present.
Apologies
There were no apologies.
Consideration of draft agenda
Hon Reddy proposed that an item on the communication between the Committee Members be included in the agenda. The agenda was approved with the additional item as proposed by Hon Reddy.
Meeting report
Reports
(i) Committee Section
Ms Borchard submitted a report on matters before Committees and gave an overview of the progress made since the last meeting. She stated that there were 31 Bills before Parliament of which 17 were from Executive Members and 14 from Private Members. She further reported that there were 8 Statutory Appointments and 13 petitions before Committees.
Regarding the Tobacco Products and  Electronic Delivery Systems Control Bill, she reported that the motion of desirability would be considered by the Portfolio Committee on Health on 11 March 2026. Regarding the  Prevention of Illegal Eviction from and Unlawful Occupation of Land Amendment Bill, the Portfolio Committee on Human Settlements have withdrawn the consideration of the motion of desirability pending further consultations. The Portfolio Committee on Mineral and Petroleum Resources aimed to finish the South African National Petroleum Company Bill in the Third Term of 2026.  The Standing Committee on Appropriations would receive briefings from the Financial and Fiscal Commission and the Parliamentary Budget Office on the  Special Appropriation Bill on 6 March 2026 and it aimed to finalise the report on the Bill on 25 March 2026. Regarding the 2026 Fiscal Framework, the Standing Committee on Finance would hold public hearings on 10 March 2026 and it aimed to consider its report on 17 March 2026.
Regarding statutory appointments, Ms Borchard reported that the Portfolio Committee on Women, Youth and Persons with Disabilities adopted its report Recommending candidates for appointment to the Board of the National Council on Gender-Based Violence and Femicide on 4 March 2026. Furthermore, the Committee would commence with the shortlisting process for the appointment of Commissioners on the Commission for Gender Equality on 24 March 2026.
Regarding the petition from Hon N Nolutshungu, on behalf of the Western Cape Network on Disability, calling for the amendment and enforcement of the National Land Transport Act, she reported that the Portfolio Committee on Transport would start dealing with the petition towards the end of March 2026, pending the amendment of its programme.
(ii)        Bills Office
Dr Mbatha concurred with the report from Committees Section and reported that three Money Bills have been introduced by the Minister of Finance on 25 February 2026 from which the Special Appropriation Bill has been referred to the Standing Committee on Appropriations. The Division of Revenue Bill and the Appropriation Bill would be referred to Committees after the consideration of 2026 Fiscal Framework and Revenue Proposals which was scheduled for 24 March 2026.  The Traditional and Khoi-San Leadership Bill and the Public Procurement Amendment Bill were in the final process of being prepared for introduction in Parliament as soon as possible.  She also reported that a total of seven Bills were with the President for assent.
Adv Van der Merwe presented a report on legislation with Constitutional Court deadlines as follows:
Basic Conditions of Employment Act 75 of 1997
and
Unemployment Insurance Act 63 of 2001
The Department of Labour confirmed on 9 February 2026 that legislative amendments to both Acts have been developed to address the Court’s concerns. The targeted date for publication of the Bills for public comment was March 2026, and the Department targeted April 2026 for introduction.
Traditional and Khoi-San Leadership Act, 2019 (Act No. 3 of 2019)
The Bill has been certified, and the Department of Cooperative Governance and Traditional Affairs would brief the Portfolio Committee on the Bill on 10 March 2026.
Discussion:
Hon Mkhaliphi referred to the Electoral Commission Amendment Bill that was introduced in 2023 and expressed concern at the protracted period it took for Portfolio Committees to process Bills in general, often resulting in Bills lapsing. She enquired about the reasons for this after which Ms Borchard undertook to furnish the Committee with a written response on the matter. House Chairperson Frolick stated that the Portfolio Committee on Home Affairs has been seized with public hearings across South Africa on a different Bill and that the Committees Section should provide a further update at the next meeting.
Hon Reddy alluded to the timeframes used in the reports to the Committee and expressed concerns about non-specific terms like “as soon as possible” being used. He asked when exactly the Traditional and Khoi-San Leadership Bill would be introduced as there were specific areas that needed to be addressed. The Speaker stated that there was a need to focus on how Committees programmed legislation in general and to ensure that Bills enjoyed priority. She also requested House Chairperson Frolick to present the Committee with tentative proposals on how to address this matter. The Speaker also emphasised that the seriousness of the matter must be brought to the attention of Portfolio Committee Chairpersons so as to expedite the processing of Bills.
Consideration of Parliamentary Programme
Hon Dugmore presented the updated Parliamentary Programme for the First Term of 2026 and highlighted the importance of the consideration of the joint interim report on the statutory rape of young girls scheduled for 5 March 2026. He further emphasised that the recommendation of candidates to serve on the board of the National Council on Gender-Based Violence and Femicide would also be considered that afternoon and required a supporting vote of the majority of members of the Assembly (at least 201 in favour).
On 10 March 2026, the plenary would include Members’ Statements and the Statement by the Minister of Justice and Constitutional Development on the 30-year anniversary celebration of the Constitution of the Republic of South Africa. He further highlighted that there would be no motions and notices of motions on that day.
Hon Dugmore indicated that for the period 30 March to 2 April 2026 the Tuesday and Wednesday would be allocated to Committees and the Thursday would be a Constituency Day. Regarding the consideration of the Division of Revenue Bill, he indicated that the proposal was to schedule this on 21 April 2026 to allow the Standing Committee on Appropriations more time to finalise the Bill.
The Secretary to the National Assembly highlighted the proposed schedule for Members’ Training and Capacity Building.  The Speaker further encouraged members to attend the Inter-Parliamentary Union’s Webinar focusing on Building Artificial Intelligence Literacy in Parliaments that was scheduled for 1 April 2026.
Discussions:
Hon Reddy highlighted the importance of the National Assembly Members’ training schedule for 2026 and appealed that notifications in that regard be distributed as early as possible. He further enquired about the reasons for Members’ Statements not being scheduled on 12 March 2026 when the President of the Republic of South Africa would be in the House. House Chairperson Frolick stated that it has been a longstanding convention that other items are not included in the Order Paper when the President answers questions in the House, unless  it was a very urgent matter that could only be considered after the question session. The Secretary to the National Assembly explained that in terms to the Rules of the National Assembly, questions had precedence on question days. Furthermore, the President was not a member of the Assembly, therefore he was not required to remain in the House after the question session. The Speaker stated that any proposals for reviews to the Rules of the National Assembly could be forwarded for referral to the Rules Committee.
Hon Mkhaliphi expressed concerns about the quality of responses received from the Executive Members, with specific reference to the Acting Minister of Police, during question time and appealed to the Speaker to address the matter. House Chairperson Frolick stated that the under preparedness of the Executive Members created unnecessary tension in the house and that this defeated the purpose of question sessions that were meant to be interactive in nature. He continued that the matter of quality of responses needed to be taken up  with the Leader of Government Business. Hon Mkhaliphi further stated that the member of the EFF assigned to the Joint Standing Committee on Intelligence was unable to perform her responsibilities due to the vetting process not being finalised. She requested that this matter be followed up with the relevant stakeholders.
Consideration of Minutes of meeting of 19 February 2026
On the proposal of Mr V Reddy, seconded by Mr Kwankwa, the minutes of 19 February 2026 were adopted.

Matters arising
The Secretary to the National Assembly indicated that the term of the Ad Hoc Committee to review and amend the Parliamentary Villages Management Board Act would expire on 31 March 2026 and that it needed to be extended. He further stated that a motion would have to be tabled in the House in this regard during the week 10 to 13 March 2026.
He also emphasised that Party Whips would have to indicate how many of their members were present when it came to the decision on the nominations to the board of the National Council on Gender-Based Violence and Femicide.
Communication in the NA Programming Committee
Hon Reddy proposed that a WhatsApp group be established for the NAPC after which Hon Dugmore indicated that this was not necessary as proper communication channels were already in place. The Speaker concurred with Hon Dugmore and urged Committee Members to monitor their e-mails for notifications.
Announcements
None.
Closure
The meeting adjourned at 09:16.
