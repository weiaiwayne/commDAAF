Meeting summary
The Portfolio Committee on Communications and Digital Technologies convened a virtual meeting to discuss various internal agenda items. The Committee went through and agreed to its programme for meetings and activities in the first term of 2024. The Committee then considered and agreed to its 2022/23 annual activities report and a second draft of its legacy report for the Sixth Parliament. The DA said it would add to some areas not reflected in the report for fullness and accuracy.
The Committee considered a concept document on a capacity-building programme in February and March to provide education to policymakers, increase their awareness of digital media platforms, and address concerns, such as online safety, misinformation, and public engagement, ahead of the elections.
Members voiced support for the urgency of addressing disinformation and proposed extending session durations for meaningful discussions.
The meeting concluded with the adoption of outstanding minutes.
Meeting report
Opening remarks
The Chairperson, Mr B Maneli (ANC), noted that the Committee had received an apology from the Deputy Minister of Communications and Digital Technologies. To ensure no oversight, he checked for any apologies from Members that may not have been recorded in the office.
The Chairperson assumed there were no further apologies except for the Minister's, and he proceeded to start the meeting. He hoped he was audible despite network instability.
The Chairperson then addressed the Members, emphasising the need to efficiently deal with the formalities of the meeting to avoid unnecessary delays. He reminded everyone of the rules regarding muting microphones and switching off video cameras when not speaking, especially for those experiencing network challenges.
He explained that while points of order were allowed, Members should raise their hands using their gadgets and wait to be recognised before speaking. If the Chairperson could not see a raised hand, the Committee support staff would assist in identifying the Member.
The Chairperson welcomed Members, civil society representatives, and other participants, expressing gratitude for their engagement in parliamentary affairs. He also acknowledged the support staff and observed a moment of silence for a departed Member of the Committee.
Regarding recent events, the Chairperson mentioned South Africa's representation at the International Court of Justice regarding the Palestinian issue and the national football team's – Bafana Bafana - match against Morocco. He encouraged support for the team, reflecting on the country's history of unity and victory.
Committee Programme Term 1 2024
The Chairperson highlighted the flexibility of the programme to accommodate public interest matters and legislative processes. He explained the scheduling of discussions on various topics, including the South African Broadcasting Corporation (SABC) and the separation of time for engaging with the State Information Technology Agency (SITA) to address all relevant matters thoroughly.
The Chairperson elaborated on the other agenda items, such as the scenario report from the Committee content advisor and the consideration of engagement with media platforms to address misinformation, especially in the context of upcoming elections.
Concluding, the Chairperson invited Members to raise any issues or concerns regarding the agenda and documentation. Assuming no objections, he proceeded with the presentation and discussion of the programme for adoption.
The Members adopted the agenda with no objections and the meeting proceeded.
Draft Committee Annual Activities Report 2022/23
Mr J Medupe, Committee Content Advisor, then proceeded to provide an overview of the annual report.
In his presentation, Mr Medupe outlined the report's various sections. These included details on the overview by the Chairperson, the Membership of the Committee, and the emphasis on the mandate. He also discussed bills referred to the Committee, including support and processing, as well as finances, budget votes, and fiscal framework considerations.
Continuing with the report, Mr Medupe mentioned the community strategic plan (which was non-applicable), conferences attended (none), and internal study trips and undertakings (also none). However, there were attempts to take study trips. He further highlighted the Committee meetings held throughout the year and the finalisation meeting with public hearings.
Mr Medupe then touched upon topics such as the conservation of Section 32 reports (none), consideration of annual reports from various entities, and information on entities reporting to the Department. He also noted any applications and activities not approved, mentioning that there were none.
There was only one cancelled meeting in December 2022.
Moving on, Mr Medupe discussed the oversight visit to the Free State and noted that no sponsorships were received. He mentioned papers referred to the Committee for consideration, personnel referred to the Committee (none), and Sub-Committee work, including the appointment of the SABC Board.
The Chairperson thanked everyone and proceeded to address a matter regarding sponsorship. He mentioned the need to check if there were any numbers currently on record and to make any necessary corrections or deletions before further discussion.
The Chairperson emphasised the importance of clarity regarding sponsorship, particularly in distinguishing between sponsorship and stakeholder engagement, mentioning a stakeholder engagement that took place in Gauteng – that might have been a sponsorship. He requested clarification to ensure transparency and accuracy in the information provided.
Further, the Chairperson asked for clarification on whether certain activities should be considered as sponsorship or stakeholder engagement. He sought input from the Members to ensure that the information reflected in the report was accurate.
Discussion
Ms N Mazzone (DA) said the DA had yet to review the report in its study group. Therefore, she requested that they reserve their rights regarding the upcoming vote.
The Chairperson acknowledged this and assured her there was still an opportunity to provide input. He mentioned that the report had to be sent to the House but reiterated that Ms Mazzone's point about reserving their right was noted. The Chairperson emphasised that the report aimed to address any gaps, including those that the DA might want to submit. He encouraged Ms Mazzone to highlight anything she believed had been omitted.
The Members agreed with the final version of the Annual Report except for the DA.
Draft Committee Legacy Report 2019 - 2024
Mr Medupe provided an overview of the changes made between the first and second drafts of the report. He mentioned that subsections 1 and 3 had collapsed into one, as did subsections 2 and 4. This consolidation resulted in four main sections.
In subsection 2 of the key highlights, a paragraph regarding the impact of COVID-19 misinformation management was added. Additionally, in subsection 4, a sentence emphasising the importance of the appointment of the full board of the SABC was included.
In the document's main body, under the title "Context and Background," a paragraph addressing South Africa's societal challenges and issues such as unemployment, inequality, and corruption, was added. Further, the issue of vandalism and ageing infrastructure was highlighted, emphasising the need for policy intervention and law enforcement.
In the section detailing the methods of work of the Committee, a sentence regarding oversight programming and declined oversight applications was added. Additionally, updates were made regarding key policy developments, legislation and international agreements.
Under the key statistics section, information on international agreements processed and petitions considered by the Committee was populated.
In the section on legislation, a comment was added to indicate bills (SABC Bill in particular) still being processed by the Committee.
Regarding oversight trips, a point was added to ensure fair allocation of the budget for study tours and international trips for all committees.
Updates were made to the section on international agreements to reflect agreements processed during the current administration.
Finally, under the section on other matters referred by the Speaker, additions were made regarding various communications and notices received by the Committee.
Discussion
The Chairperson reminded Members that they had received the first draft of the report last year and now had the second draft, which had taken into account submissions from the content advisor and support staff. He invited Members to make any corrections or provide further information if necessary.
Ms Mazzone raised concerns regarding certain issues that the Democratic Alliance (DA) had raised but were not included in the report. She suggested drafting a formal letter outlining these concerns to ensure clarity and avoid confusion. Ms Mazzone proposed sending the letter to the Chairperson's office, who would then handle it with the Committee staff according to their usual practice.
She emphasised the importance of documenting these concerns in writing to ensure complete clarity. Ms Mazzone sought the Chairperson's agreement on this approach and asked for a timeframe within which to submit the letter. She assured the Chairperson that she would ensure the document's timely delivery for consideration by the Committee. She concluded by acknowledging that the report was fair overall but believed that certain important points should be added or adjusted.
The Chairperson thanked the Member for her contributions and sought confirmation if any other Members wished to speak. Not receiving any further responses, the Chairperson acknowledged that the submission process could proceed accordingly.
He clarified that since it was the second draft and not yet the final draft for adoption, there was still room for further adjustments based on feedback. The Chairperson sought confirmation from the content advisor regarding the timeframe for incorporating any changes received before the final report was tabled, to which the content advisor agreed.
Ms Mazzone expressed her appreciation and assurance that the document would be submitted within the indicated timeframe.
The other Members agreed with Ms Mazzone's suggestion and the Chairperson's proposal.
Digital Platform: Series of Parliamentary Outreach
Mr Medupe explained the concept document developed in collaboration with various digital media platforms. He outlined the background of the document, noting that it stemmed from a symposium held last year on misinformation during elections. Following the symposium, meetings were held with the platforms to agree on the way forward, resulting in the concept document being presented to the Members.
The goal of the engagement outlined in the document was to provide education to policymakers, increasing their awareness of digital media platforms and addressing concerns raised during the symposium, such as online safety, misinformation, and public engagement.
Mr Medupe then presented the proposed structure and format of the workshop sessions. Each session would last approximately 45 minutes, consisting of a presentation followed by a question-and-answer session. Platforms would be allocated one session each per week by the parliamentary Committee. Proposed topics included safety and security, political activities, content integrity, and election security.
He also provided proposed dates for the sessions, starting in February - 26 March 2024, with each platform allocated specific dates for their presentations.
In conclusion, Mr Medupe sought the Members' approval and further input on the proposed concept document.
Discussion
The Chairperson thanked Mr Medupe for introducing the subject and expressed his willingness to hear from other Members who wished to contribute. He emphasised the importance of seeking agreement from the Members to proceed with the proposed concept document. The Chairperson noted that the content advisor and Committee support would then be tasked with processing all letters pertaining to external individuals attending the meetings.
Ms Mazzone indicated her full agreement with the urgency of addressing disinformation, misinformation, and the impact of artificial intelligence (AI), considering them significant threats to democracy globally. She emphasised the importance of taking action, particularly with numerous countries having elections scheduled for the year 2024.
Regarding the proposed timeframes for engagement with social media platforms, Ms Mazzone voiced her support. However, she suggested considering extending the session durations beyond the allocated 45 minutes due to the gravity of the issues being discussed.
Further, Ms Mazzone noted that South Africa is a member of the Commonwealth Parliamentary Union, which recently released a handbook addressing misinformation, disinformation, and the threat of AI to democracy. She recommended obtaining a
digital copy of this handbook
, spanning approximately 32 to 33 pages, to better equip the Committee for engaging with social media platforms on this critical issue.
The Chairperson proposed continuing the capacity-building programme and finalising the proposed dates for planning purposes. He explained that the chosen dates were informed by past attendance patterns at the Wits Learning Information Networking Knowledge (LINK) Centre programme and the fact that it is an election year, where party programmes may affect Members' availability. The aim was to select dates aligned with the Committee's meeting schedule to ensure maximum Member participation.
The Chairperson then asked for any objections to proceeding with the capacity-building programme and finalising the dates. He noted the importance of submitting this information to the Office of the House Chair for further action.
Mr R Mdakane (ANC) expressed his support for the programme and the proposed timing, suggesting that the duration be extended to an hour if possible. He highlighted the importance of having sufficient time for meaningful discussions on communication-related issues, as the previous workshop was insightful but time-constrained. He emphasised the value of learning from such sessions, particularly for those who are not communication experts. However, he also acknowledged the need to balance the time allocated for the programme with other Committee responsibilities.
The Chairperson noted that the issue regarding the duration of the programme will be examined further, as it falls within the Committee's purview. Since it aligns with their schedule, they can make adjustments accordingly. Assuming there were no objections, he understood that the proposal was accepted.
Moving on to the next agenda item, the Chairperson indicated that the meeting would proceed with the approval of the minutes in the usual manner, with Members providing feedback on each page if necessary. If there are no comments or amendments to be made, the minutes will be adopted with the mover and seconder, who were present during the meeting under review.
Adoption of outstanding minutes
Minutes dated 12 December 2023
Ms Mazzone moved to accept the minutes, which Mr T Gumbu (ANC) seconded.
The minutes were duly adopted.
The Chairperson expressed gratitude to the Members for their participation despite their other commitments. He noted that procedural matters would be addressed, and the programme would be implemented as planned. The Chairperson also encouraged Members to support the national football team, Bafana Bafana, and concluded the meeting by thanking everyone and confirming that everything remains unchanged.
Meeting adjourned.
