Title: Provincial Treasury measures to mitigate and prevent high department vacancy rates; Committee Reports
Date: 26 Mar 2025
Committee: 
URL: https://pmg.org.za/committee-meeting/40466/
============================================================

Meeting Summary
Video
The Public Accounts Committee (PAC) convened to address key issues related to employee wellbeing amid high vacancy rates at the Provincial Treasury. The Provincial Treasury presented measures to mitigate workforce strain, including budget reprioritisation, critical post-recruitment, technology integration, flexible work arrangements, and employee wellness programmes. Planned initiatives involve filling unfunded posts, training, cultural engagement, and mentorship. The Treasury’s vacancy rate stands at 23% (against a provincial average of 36.3%), with efforts underway to expedite recruitment, though challenges persist due to competitive salary offers from municipalities and other organisations.
Members raised concerns over the 82-day recruitment timeline, salary competitiveness, technology’s role in reducing mundane tasks, and the effectiveness of internship programmes. Questions also focused on fiscal consolidation’s impact on staffing, union engagement, and career progression for interns. The Treasury clarified that technology (including AI pilots) aims to augment and not replace staff, while flexible work policies have boosted productivity. The Department of the Premier highlighted labour relations frameworks, including quarterly union forums.
On unresolved resolutions (4.1 and 4.2), Members debated whether to await the Auditor-General’s investigation or hear directly from a whistleblower. Consensus favoured inviting the whistleblower on 23 April 2025, pending procedural fairness considerations.
Meeting report
Briefing by the Provincial Treasury on Employee Wellbeing Management Measures
Ms Deidre Baartman, Western Cape Minister of Finance, made brief remarks before the formal presentation. She said they would focus on human resources, vacancies and the filling of those within the Department. She said the latter had been her focus since she took office, to reduce the workload of the current staff complement. Support and capacitation of the teams were equally important, and they do not just include training but also the effective utilisation of technology to reduce the workload.
Ms Julinda Gantana, Head of Department (HOD), Western Cape Treasury, then led the presentation. The presentation focused on the following areas with details:
- Measures implemented, including budget reprioritisation, critical post-recruitment, technology integration, flexible work arrangements and the Employee Assistance Programme;
It then outlined planned measures, which include the application to fill additional unfunded posts through the budget process, training and development, cultural and employee engagement sessions, and mentorship, amongst others.

Discussion
Ms B Van Minnen (DA) began by asking what the vacancy rate was in the Provincial Treasury compared to other departments. Were there specific reasons for the vacancy rate, or was it due to natural attrition? Looking at the graduate and internship programme in the Department, an impression was created that there were difficulties in recruiting suitable candidates. Was this found to be the case?
Mr L Van Wyk (DA) sought clarity on the 82 business days it takes to get appointments in place, which seemed quite lengthy. He further inquired about the province’s salary levels in the context of attracting and retaining professionals. He also wished to know the Department’s view on the debate about reducing employee costs by using technology in certain functions.
Ms P Lekker (ANC) asked about the paid internships at the Department, the extent to which the matriculants recruited are competent to perform functions where there have been skilled vacancies. She also enquired if there was a timeframe attached to the application to fill unfunded posts through the budget process. She noted the 44 posts that were advertised, and asked out of how many were these from.
The Chairperson asked about the interrelations between the internship and bursary programmes. How many applications were received for the internship, and how is the recruitment managed? Did the Department see more productivity and improved employee wellbeing from the flexible work arrangements? He cautioned against employee burnout when workloads increase because people have been poached away from the Department by other organisations. Is the 23 percent vacancy rate an acceptable norm within the country?
Mr B Petrus (PA) wished to know what happens to the interns after completing the programme.
Ms N Nkondlo (ANC) sought clarity on the ‘fiscal consolidation’ mentioned by the HOD, and its implications from a human resource perspective. Has it led to any re-organisation of the departmental structure? She also wished to understand the notion of the use of technology, and how the Department determined what ‘mundane’ work is. Have they already scoped which functions would be moved over to technology and the implications for staff members who were responsible for those tasks? She asked to if the Department was able to get feedback from employees on their wellness through service providers and how it was dealt with. Is there a union within the Department? If so, why had they not given feedback on engagements with the union, in as far as labour relations are concerned? She also wished to find out the percentage of interns that had been absorbed into the organisation, as well as the Department’s career progression strategies. She asked that this information be shared with the Committee.
Responses
The HOD responded that the Department has an approved posts structure of 336, with the vacancy rate currently at 23 percent. The application for additional posts was successful.
Ms Naadia Ismail, Director: Strategic and Operational Management Support, said that the vacancy rate was favourable when compared to other departments, and they had one of the lower rates in the provincial government. Skilled professionals tend to leave for more lucrative salary offers, especially to municipalities which offered better pay. There have been instances where some turned these down, opting to stay with the Department because of potential for growth. The bursary programmes target previously disadvantaged individuals, combined with academic achievement and potential and financial need. There was a pattern of graduates being poached by other organisations after completion, however, due to salary levels within the provincial government. The National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) was also a competitor because of the bursary packages they offer, which include accommodation and other perks.
She went on to say that 82 days for recruitment may seem a long period, but there are processes that must be followed, which include advertisement right up to the Director-General's and Premier’s concurrence. Paid interns are usually fresh out of matric and not expected to perform functions of vacant posts, instead graduate interns are given the opportunity to contribute to those roles, under the mentorship of senior staff members. She also said the 44 posts advertised are all funded and have been assessed by the COE Committee as critical and must be filled. The application rates for bursaries and internships were in the hundreds and they did not struggle to find suitable candidates. Flexible work arrangements have found favour amongst employees and contributed to improved productivity. Graduate bursary and internship programmes are interrelated, offered for a period of up to four years, with the agreements entered with candidates to work back the equivalent period for a maximum of two years so that they acquire some skills and experience. A number of internships have gone through the ranks, and there were success stories throughout the provincial government.
The HOD said, because of fiscal consolidation some two to three years ago, the Department had to lead by example and make cuts in terms of the equitable share. This came at a cost and included not filling the personal assistant positions where they were vacant. The current review then looks at the administrative tasks that can be piloted for automated assistance. She said they recognise that technology works well alongside people. She went on to say that all unions are represented within the Department. The Provincial Treasury had not restructured since 2009. There is now a need to revisit the current structure in line with the Department’s goals.
Ms Karen Scholtz, People Management, Department of the Premier, said that each department has an Institutional Management and Labour Committee (IMLC) in place that meets on quarterly basis, as well as a collective bargaining forum where all unions are represented.
Ms Ismail said that there was no intention to use artificial intelligence (AI) to replace people but rather to embrace it for all the advantages it brings. It helps with reducing repetitive tasks, analysing data and generating reports and minutes in short periods. There will be on-boarding of pilot programmes of these platforms within the Department in the near future.
Minister Baartman said that the training and development, as shown in the reports, is part of the bigger strategy to empower staff members. There have also been concerted efforts to ensure the filling of critical vacancies and taking these through Cabinet processes, as well as the supply chain structures in the province. The Department has been able to predict the economic growth scenarios for the province, which helps with planning. She said that the flexibility of working from home offers the space to ensure individual productivity.
The HOD said they would later share the information on career progression and the number of years those who served longest had spent in various positions. The Department can always accommodate all interns, so they have been placed with other departments and municipalities where possible. All of them do, however, work back the period of two years. There has been progress with filling the infrastructure post and other middle management vacancies in that unit. Part of the management of career progression include creating an environment where people want to stay despite salary levels.
It was also said that the average vacancy rate in the Western Cape government is 36.3 percent, much higher than the Provincial Treasury. Research has shown however that if the rate is above 20 percent, significant strain begins to be felt. The structure and functional logic play a role in how the departments function under those circumstances. The current recruitment drive would bring the numbers down significantly.
Consideration and Adoption of Minutes
The Committee went on to consider its minutes of 12 February 2025.
The minutes were adopted without amendments.
Resolutions/Actions
The Chairperson gave feedback on previous resolutions and said that only Resolutions 4.1 and 4.2 were outstanding. The procedural advice had been shared with the Members, in writing. He said that the two options there were to either wait for the outcome of the investigation of the Auditor-General or call in the whistleblower to come present to the Committee. He noted that there were allegations against the whistleblower as well but invited the Committee to decide on the way forward.
Mr Petrus said he agreed that the whistleblower be called in to state their side of the story.
Ms Nkondlo asked what the basis was for the advice to await the outcome of the Auditor-General process. The right of the whistleblower must not be infringed simply because they are being investigated. She supported the view of the Chairperson for the whistleblower to be invited to appear before the Committee.
Ms Lekker supported this view in the spirit of fairness and ensuring a just process.
Ms Van Minnen said the Committee must be careful not to use such language as ‘investigation’ and set a precedent for parallel investigations.
The Chairperson said the Committee would decide after listening to Mr Cloete what the way forward is. To avoid the setting of a precedent, cases will be dealt with on their individual merits.
The Chairperson said it seemed that Members were on the same page. He said the next available date on the programme is 23 April 2025 when the matter could be dealt with, after the business scheduled for that day is completed.
The Committee agreed to this.
The Chairperson said that the third quarter activities report would be emailed to Members and dealt with in the next meeting.
The meeting was adjourned.
