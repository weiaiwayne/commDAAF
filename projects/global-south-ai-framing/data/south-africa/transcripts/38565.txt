Meeting Summary
Video
In this virtual meeting, the Portfolio Committee on Communications and Digital Technologies participated in a capacity-building programme conducted by Meta Inc and TikTok. The Committee was briefed on the platforms' approach to elections, and how to use platforms and political ads. The Committee will be briefed on safety, security, and content moderation in later sessions.
The Department of Communications and Technologies (DCDT) briefed the Committee on the progress of the broadcasting digital migration (BDM) programme.
The second phase of ongoing industry consultations led to securing commitment by the main role players. So far, there has been confirmation of funding secured from the National Treasury for programme implementation towards the BDM conclusion. The service level agreements with state-owned entities and broadcasters have been concluded. A decoder supply service provider was appointed, and an order was placed for an additional 200 000 decoder kits for installation in the outstanding registered households. The State Information Technology Agency’s (SITA) services were secured and ready to launch an online registration application system for indigent households. It has reached an in-principle agreement with the broadcasters for phase two of the analogue switch-off (ASO), covering the phases and sequence to switch off the outstanding 195 analogue services.
The Department reported that the implementation of the ASO process, with its preceding precursor activities, is heavily reliant on the full collaboration of internal and external stakeholders, including public and private broadcasters. To mitigate the risk, state-owned entities and broadcasters recurrently carry out continuous engagements, planning coordination, and implementation to limit deviations from the planned final ASO. The ASO implementation plan has been agreed upon by stakeholders and free-to-air broadcasters, giving the confidence to meet the December 2024 ASO timeline commitment.
The Committee discussed interventions to turn around the State Information Technology Agency (SITA).
Members said that SITA is the reason for the bad reputation of various government departments, especially for service delivery. Members often hear complaints about the entity in all portfolio committees. The entity has had a negative impact on the lives of South Africans, and there has been no progress in its performance.
Further, they pointed out that SITA is an important structure in the entire department. It is supposed to ensure or provide confidence that the entire communication sector is protected against criminals and people who interfere for various reasons. They highlighted that SITA should not become a permanent site where quick intervention is needed. It should look at the top management of SITA and the culture of things at the operational level.
The Minister of Communications and Digital Technology shared the concerns raised by Members and indicated that the ministerial task team, which was appointed to address this, had made a significant difference.
Members were informed that there had been a change in SITA's focus. It focuses not only on technology but also on people and processes. Technology is not the only thing that provides change; people and processes also contribute significantly to this. SITA has been arranging training with the School of Government. The plan is to use the people with the required skills to close the required vacancies. There is a long-term intent behind everything, and it ensures sustainability. One cannot run a continuous sequence of interventions. The interventions are intended to get SITA to a new steady state. There are specific timeframes for this, ensuring that the management team is capable and has the necessary skills to deal with challenges going forward. Some new technologies are based on a smaller customer demand. This is in progress, and within the next six months, it will get all the positions it needs.
The Minister also took the Committee through the SITA judgement. It was held that the matter was urgent. It was declared that the filing of the application for leave to appeal suspends the operation and execution of the order granted on 02 February 2024, pending the outcome of any appeal processes filed by the applicant.

Progress update on the broadcasting digital migration project
Ms Nonkqubela Thathakahle Jordan-Dyani, Director-General, DCDT, took the Committee through the presentation.
The last update to the Committee focused on the outcome of the consultations with stakeholders, particularly broadcasters, towards realising the agreed two-stage ASO approach. The Minister and the DCDT engaged with stakeholders between May and June 2023. The last update included a report on the registrations and installations that enabled the broadcasters' authorisation for the first stage of ASO. Since the last update, additional progress has been achieved on the two-stage approach towards the conclusion of ASO.
The ministerial consultations with the industry culminated in a two-step approach to ASO: switch off analogue services above the 694MHz band by 31 July 2023, temporarily accommodate some of the services to below 694MHz, and finally switch off by 31 December 2024. Since the consultations and implementation of phase one resolutions, 123 analogue services were switched off by 31 July 2024. The remaining analogue services, 195 in total, are scheduled to be switched off by 31 December 2024.
The awareness strategy developed by the South African Broadcasting Corporation (SABC) and the DCDT since the last Committee update, culminated in the finalisation of service level agreements (SLAs) for consumer awareness, led by the SABC. For the period up to 30 September 2022, there were 1 554 309 applications registered for the government decoder subsidy-forming a baseline for required installations and the subsequent determination of ASO. For the period of 01 October 2022 to 31 July 2023, an additional 138 132 registrations were captured. The cumulative registration total for the period to 31 July 2023 was, therefore, 1 692 441 registrations. The South African Post Office (SAPO) has recorded a total of 24 783 registrations across the country between 01 August 2023 and 29 February 2024. Up to July 2023, a total of 1 256 099 installations were completed by Universal Service and Access Agency of South Africa and Sentech. For the period 01 August 2023 to 29 February 2024, Sentech has completed a total of 35 585 installations. To date, a cumulative 1 291 684 installations have been completed.
There have been 1 717 224 registrations and 1 291 684 installations. Following the confirmation of funding on 31 October 2023, the Universal Service and Access Agency of South Africa (USAASA) placed orders of 200 000 additional decoders required to cater for the already registered households. Although National Treasury confirmed that USAASA could retain funds allocated in 2023/24 for the programme. Unfortunately, delays in the confirmation of funds impacted the programme's implementation between April 2023 and October 2023. Nevertheless, the confirmation enabled USAASA to finalise SLAs and appoint entities and broadcasters.
The implementation of the ASO process, with its preceding precursor activities, is heavily reliant on the full collaboration of internal and external stakeholders, including public and private broadcasters. To mitigate the risk, continuous engagements, planning coordination, and implementation are recurrently carried out by state-owned entities and broadcasters to limit deviations from the planned final ASO. The ASO implementation plan has been agreed upon by stakeholders and free-to-air broadcasters, giving the confidence to meet the December 2024 ASO timeline commitment. SLAs are in place to ensure the delivery of activities that precede ASO.

Performance information
The amended annual targets included reviewing the SITA model and developing a recommendations roadmap, implementing 90% of the service improvement plan, and implementing 100% of the supply-chain management dispensation pilot for one selected security services department. The amended quarterly targets consisted of an unqualified audit opinion with no material audit findings on non-financial performance information and an unqualified audit opinion with 10% fewer findings than in 2022/23 on financial performance information.
Projected performance results for quarter four
Following the outcome of the 2022/23 financial year audit, management needed to review the targets relating to the audit outcomes, as they were deemed incorrectly captured as they currently refer to the previous financial year audit outcomes rather than the current year being audited. DCDT revised and approved the annual performance plan, and tabling at the Committee is expected by the end of February 2024.
There were 18 targets in total, seven of which were low-risk targets, four medium-risk targets, and seven high-risk targets.
The presentation also covered the Minister's outcomes commitments internally (such as broadband), externally (such as rationalisation of benefits) and what still needs to be done (such as capacitation of organisation). The presentation provided a detailed table regarding digitising frontline government e-services.
SITA’s proposed approach to service penalties
SITA incurred SLA penalties amounting to R935 547 68 from 01 April 2023 to 31 December 2023, which is 0.026% against a billing of R3 549 519 621 81. These penalties underscore SITA’s commitment to upholding performance against SLA. SITA is also looking towards further increasing the current SLA penalties where possible but will remain within the 0.5% ministerial commitment, reinforcing the determination to improve service provision. The revised penalty position for availability service levels for hosting and network services will be implemented from 01 April 2024, targeting all contracted clients.
SITA has incorporated benchmarking into its packaging and pricing processes for all services to ensure competitive pricing. Should clients present SITA with market pricing, SITA will endeavour to analyse and match or beat these prices for the same or more value (like for like or better) in a sustainable manner.
Ministerial task team
In December 2023, the Minister appointed a ministerial task team to address complaints of procurement backlogs by SITA affecting the delivery of information technology services for national and provincial departments as well as some of the local municipalities such as the City of Cape Town. The Ministry has received numerous complaints from other ministries at national and provincial departments across the country, regarding SITA’s failure to fulfil its legislated mandate to provide information technology (IT), information systems and related services. SITA’s failure to respond to their customers’ IT requirements has affected various departments’ service delivery obligations to South Africans.
It is worth noting that the ministerial task team started its work in December 2023 when most government departments and entities, including SITA, were about to close for the festive break. However, the ministerial task team managed to get a significant amount of work done during that time, and it continued as the officials resumed after the holidays.
Some of the challenges identified included slow turnaround times for supply-chain management, a lack of capacity in human bodies and required skills, and a lack of accountability and proper reporting structures. Some of the recommendations have been fully or partially implemented, such as appointing an acting chief procurement officer and a service provider from the existing supply-chain management resources. The presentation also mentioned some of the projects that are in the pipeline to improve supply-chain management inefficiencies.

This is the focus point for the next six months. In the presentation, there is a slide on cybersecurity. SITA has a framework that established itself. However, the framework needs to be industrialised further and be shared with other customers. There is a lot of work to be done. It is not only about the technology but also methodologies, process and philosophical worldviews, diversity, artificial intelligence and all those technology concepts. A bigger forum discussion is required because it has to take a South African position. Unfortunately, there is only a SITA perspective/position on it at the moment.
Mr Dzengwa said that SITA is attending to the transversal panel issue for the Western Cape. SITA has been engaging with the Western Cape leadership, such as the Director-General, the Department of Health and Basic Education senior officials, and legal representatives. It has agreed on a joint solution so that it does not involve the court to oppose one another’s government. The Western Cape had requested some documents, and getting the documents had been a battle, which created a complicated relationship. During a meeting, the documents were distributed within an hour. SITA is thinking of being proactive and responsive but doing things differently to ensure it is alive to client issues and concerns as long as it is within the ambit of good governance and the law. SITA has found a solution with the Western Cape Government and even proceeded to discuss other priorities that need attention.
The Chairperson informed Mr Dzengwa that he got muted and should continue with the other priorities that need attention.
Mr Dzengwa said that broadband connectivity was one of the other priorities that needed attention in the Western Cape. The current contract expires in October, so SITA is working on this item and will deliver it as late as June. There are issues and challenges with power outages from generators at facilities, but there is engagement to ensure the facilities are up and running. A similar attitude and approach is taken with other provinces. There is engagement with other entities, and SITA will engage with the minister as soon as all the information has been received. SITA understands the risk of failing and what it brings to government services and the country. It is finding technology, human and other solutions. One of the priorities of SITA is to reduce the number of findings by the AG. This is to ensure that, over time, it moves from qualified to unqualified and then to a clean audit report. SITA is beefing up its capacity and controls. The past weekend positions for the executive of internal audit have been advertised. There is a meeting with the audit committee to further engage on these matters and the measures being put in place. The report from the ministerial task team covers some of the concerns raised about other government departments failing because SITA is failing. This is something that SITA cannot deny, and it can only proactively work to assist departments because it is not nice when funds are taken away. It is not a good thing when other government departments miss out on plans or deliver their mandates because SITA failed. He was not being defensive but explained that in some instances, government departments would process items and then, towards the end, issue a letter cancelling the item with SITA. SITA is then left hamstrung, and this amounts to wasteful and fruitless expenditure that SITA incurs. Instead of complaining, SITA is working with other departments and engaging around some of those things. The issue of poor specking results in delays when it comes to audit outcomes. There are engagements with other government departments to ensure that these issues are being dealt with so that the item has been properly closed by the time evaluation is supposed to happen. Sometimes, during the evaluation process, the quality and calibre of people sent by government departments cause delays and challenges because of common misinterpretations and misunderstandings. The issue around the cost of services has been a bugbear. Sometimes people want a Ferrari kind of service but have a Toyota kind of budget. This creates issues. Departments are willing to pay a minimal amount, but the service they require exceeds that cost. He was not trying to be defensive but explaining the situation holistically. SITA is taking note of the comments and advice from the Committee on some of these issues. Hopefully, there will be overall performance improvements when SITA returns to the Committee again.
Ms Jordan-Dyani said that the DCDT is working hard to ensure that it creates greater value for local manufacturing. The DCDT is developing programmes. There were programmes in the past. The information communication technology (ICT) SMME strategy also looks at encouraging the uptake of locally produced mobile devices, routers, laptops and audio-visual content. The DCDT is well aware of the current status within the country. From an industry perspective, there is greater input and ad exports. The DCDT wants to change the scenario and has been looking at areas in which it can increase opportunities. The BDM project is intentional in ensuring that local installers are trained not only for the set-top box installation but also for the Internet of Things. This helps with adapting from moving from one area to the next such as electronic products in the household. The goal is to export those skills to other areas, such as solar panels. There are opportunities under the SA Connect programme, which has been opened to ensure that all installers, SMMEs, entrepreneurs in the space and internet service providers who hold or do not hold licenses participate. Over 79 SMMEs are participating in the SA Connect programme. The goal is to increase this number under the BDM project for local content and the next batch of devices. The goal is to ensure this is a continuous programme, not just a once-off. The ICT SMME strategy should continue over and above this. In November, the Cabinet approved the Next Generation Spectrum Policy. This policy is deliberate in terms of turnaround time and ensuring that past mistakes are not repeated. The regulator must then set aside the spectrum used by the state digital infrastructure companies, namely Sentech and Broadband Infraco. The policy recognises that the building of infrastructure is capital-intensive. To ensure the inclusivity of SMMEs in the market, it has to set aside the spectrum for SMMEs. Hopefully, this will create and ensure transformation in the sector. The DCDT is working with SITA, National Treasury and the Department of Trade, Industry and Competition to ensure an increased threshold in terms of local content and the uptake of various programmes that are being implemented.
The Chairperson said that the matters raised would be addressed during those meetings with the Minister in this Committee. The Committee appreciated the two presentations and the interventions. The interventions should be able to deliver what the DCDT and its entities are committing to. SITA should not become a permanent site where quick intervention is needed. It should look at the top management of SITA and the culture of things at the operational level. This is the deeper understanding that the Committee was expressing. It is not just about bringing SMMEs on board but also about bringing proper definitions regarding the digital economy. It is about bringing in new entrants to the digital economy that allows spectrum benefits to SMMEs. Some would not have invested in such areas, and those that would have to carry this task without the necessary resources. The New Generation Spectrum Policy will help to simplify participation. It is one thing to create space for that participation and another if the condition excludes someone. The Committee appreciated the fact that there are consultations with stakeholders, customers and other applications. Some branches of the South African Post Offices are closing, and people do not have access. He suggested that the Minister make use of the Parliament Constituency Offices to ensure that information gets out there. He mentioned that he had attended a workshop by the DCDT, and indigent versus pensioners were conflicted most of the time. In the main, people who were pensioners and not really indigent were asked to make applications. There is an amount that the indigent qualifies for. So, even those who were trying to spread the message may not have heard the message quite properly. Hence, using these platforms will ensure the message goes out and ease the process long before it commences. The legal opinion, having a contract, and the judgment close that point. Members must respect that there is separation of powers which must be protected. The executive branch is held accountable, and the legislature plays its role. Every action that is taken must be within the law.
