Title: Executive Undertakings Progress Report: Department of Home Affairs (with Minister)
Date: 18 Jun 2025
Committee: 
URL: https://pmg.org.za/committee-meeting/41027/
============================================================

Meeting Summary
Video
In this virtual meeting, the Select Committee received a progress report on the executive undertakings that the Minister of Home Affairs made at the National Council of Provinces (NCOP) plenary on 17 July 2024.
These undertakings aim to enhance the Department of Home Affairs (DHA) role as an economic enabler through implementation of Operation Vulindlela’s reforms, clearing the backlog in the processing of permits, ending system downtime at DHA offices, reviewing queue management practices at DHA offices, resolving the stalled construction of modernised offices, and updating the security features of Smart ID cards and passports.
The Select Committee acknowledged the progress made on some of the initiatives that have been completed, particularly the upgrading of the Employment Services of South Africa (ESSA) database, the introduction of a Trusted Employer Scheme (TES), and the rollout of the eVisa system.
A Member raised the issue of improving the Branch Appointment Bookings System for Smart ID and passport applications. Only around 30 bank branches are offering these services. To address the high demand and limited availability, DHA plans to add 100 more branches shortly, with a long-term goal of expanding to 1000 branches over the strategic plan period.
Members also raised concerns about the delayed construction of modernised offices in Thohoyandou (Limpopo) and KwaDukuza (KwaZulu-Natal), as well as the disparity in services between urban and rural areas.
Meeting report
Opening remarks
The Chairperson said that from time to time, the Select Committee will review and track the commitments made by Members of the Executive. In this meeting, the Select Committee will scrutinise the executive undertakings made by the Minister of Home Affairs shortly after taking office last year. He requested Members to consider the agenda.
Mr P Noe (ANC, Free State) moved for the adoption of the agenda; Mr N Gotsell (DA, Western Cape) seconded this.
Minister’s introductory remarks
Dr Leon Schreiber, Minister of Home Affairs, recalled the first budget speech that he made before the National Council of Provinces (NCOP). It was about his vision for the Department of Home Affairs (DHA) for the 7th Administration. He said that much of this was centred on the role of digital transformation in addressing the service delivery issues that people experience. He emphasised that there are no silver bullets or magic wands to resolve all the issues at once.
He believed this was a good opportunity for DHA to check in with the Select Committee on its progress in fulfilling the executive undertakings. He said that DHA has made exciting progress. He also noted additional milestones that DHA has achieved over the 11 months, such as its work on the Digital Trusted Tour Operator Scheme (TTOS) and expanded Smart ID card access for naturalised citizens and permanent residents.
DHA Presentation
Mr Thulani Mavuso, Deputy Director-General (DDG): Institutional Planning and Support, DHA, provided a progress report on the six undertakings made at the NCOP plenary on
17 July 2024
:
The first undertaking refers to DHA’s role as an economic enabler by accelerating the implementation of Operation Vulindlela’s reforms.
Notable progress was made to transform the economy and address the binding constraints on growth during Phase 1 of Operation Vulindlela. DHA is on track to modernise IT systems, introduce automation, and expand human resources capacity in the immigration services branch.
The second undertaking refers to the clearing of the backlog in the processing of permits.
DHA’s Implementation Plan for Phase 2 of Operation Vulindlela proposes the deployment of an online waiver platform and revisions to immigration processes to expedite processing times.
The third undertaking refers to the Minister’s commitment to end system downtime.
DHA has made progress following the technical recommendations of the Council for Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR). Various projects are on track to enhance DHA’s IT infrastructure network, including the modernisation of infrastructure and network, information security enhancements, implementation of a real-time systems monitoring solution, separation of verification infrastructure, ongoing tactical interventions, and implementation of the Digital Transformation Roadmap.
The fourth undertaking refers to the Minister’s commitment to review queue management practices at DHA offices.
The current Branch Appointment Bookings System (BABS) will be enhanced to enable live notifications of clients on the status of their booking, or any system challenges an office might be facing. It will also allow the automated rescheduling of the booking.
The fifth undertaking refers to DHA’s collaboration with the Department of Public Works and Infrastructure (DPWI) to resolve the stalled construction of modernised offices in Taung (Northern Cape), Thohoyandou (Limpopo), and KwaDukuza (KwaZulu-Natal).
The Taung project is progressing, with handover to DHA anticipated in August 2025. As for the Thohoyandou and KwaDukuza projects, no progress has been made since August 2024. The contractors on these sites have been terminated by DPWI. DPWI is in the process of resuscitating these projects. DHA has also requested DPWI to transfer these two projects back to DHA for completion.
The sixth undertaking refers to the initiative to refresh and update the security features of both Smart ID cards and passports.
DHA is currently implementing an enhanced Smart Identity Document and Passport modernisation programme to reinforce the integrity and global trustworthiness of South African credentials.

Discussion
The Chairperson explained that the Select Committee is uniquely established to track and scrutinise the undertakings made by Members of the Executive across all government portfolios.
He acknowledged the progress made on some of the initiatives that have been completed, particularly the upgrading of the Employment Services of South Africa (ESSA) database and the introduction of a Trusted Employer Scheme (TES). He said that the drafting of an Implementation Plan for Operation Vulindlela Phase 2 was also a step in the right direction.
He was concerned about the delayed construction of the Thohoyandou and KwaDukuza projects and expressed satisfaction that DHA requested DPWI to return the projects for completion by DHA.
He noted the overall progress with regard to DHA’s digital transformation initiative.
Ms J Mananiso (ANC, Gauteng) noted that the Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) system is expected to be rolled out in September 2025. She requested that the Select Committee be informed of the precise rollout date.
She said that in Gauteng, the community development workers (CDWs) under the Department of Cooperative Governance and Traditional Affairs (COGTA) often collaborate with DHA. She hoped that DHA would make more use of CDWs for outreach in rural areas, especially as DHA moves towards digitisation. She explained that although research indicates smartphone access in rural areas, some individuals still lack smartphones. She suggested that CDWs could be utilised to bridge this information gap by disseminating information about DHA services, as these services are fundamental to human rights from birth to death.
She expressed appreciation for the work that DHA has carried out to date.
Mr P Phala (ANC, Limpopo) recalled that there was a previous meeting where Members raised concerns about various issues departments faced with DPWI. DPWI’s response indicated a problem with departments not adhering to the Service Level Agreements (SLAs). He asked about DHA’s relationship with DPWI. He asked if DHA had any other stalled projects due to issues with SLA’s.
Adv IM Nonkonyana (ANC, Eastern Cape) believed that an undertaking involves a commitment to do something by a specific date. If a Member of the Executive fails to fulfil an undertaking, they are required to account to the Select Committee. He agreed with the Chairperson’s acknowledgement of the progress made on some completed initiatives.
He said that the tendency of DHA to say that some matters are “on track” or “receiving attention” was discomforting, especially as it concerns immigration services. He stressed that people are in dire need of DHA’s services. He observed the disparity in DHA services between urban and rural areas, with the Cape Town branch offering “world-class” service, and rural areas often experiencing much lower quality service.
Mr M Billy (DA, KZN) said that the Select Committee is a vital component of Parliament, but it is underutilised. The Select Committee’s role is to hold the Members of the Executive accountable to the commitments they make in the House, particularly in the NCOP. He welcomed the presentation, as it provided an update on all six undertakings. He noted the good progress with Operation Vulindlela Phase 1, where 91% of reforms were completed or are on track.
He said that it was clear that steps are being taken to try and deal with the issues that exist. The roll out of the eVisa system and introduction of the points-based system (PBS) is a major leap forward in attracting investment and talent into the country. He acknowledged DHA’s leveraging of the new State Information Technology Agency (SITA) regulations to explore more cost-effective options for modernising its IT environment.
He emphasised the need to strengthen current efforts to improve BABS. He said that he received numerous complaints about the booking system, where in some instances, people could only make appointments around midnight.
The Chairperson thanked Members for making inputs and raising clarity-seeking questions. He believed that some questions, particularly Ms Mananiso’s question about the ETA system rollout date, might have to be responded to in writing. He noted that this could be done through the Committee’s Secretary, Mr Nkanyiso Mkhize.
Minister Schreiber referred to the comment on BABS. He said that there are some technical challenges related to the one-time pin (OTP) function, which is run through SITA. This is a potential candidate for resolution.
He explained that the main source of the complaints around BABS is that there are not enough booking slots. The reason for this is that there are a limited number of bank branches offering these services, resulting in a high demand for the limited slots available. There are currently only about 30 bank branches that provide these services. To resolve this, DHA aims to add 100 additional branches shortly, with a long-term goal of reaching 1000 branches over the strategic plan period.
He believed that the stalled construction projects of government buildings, particularly for routine services, are an example of why traditional “bricks and mortar” infrastructure is not the future. The shift towards digital solutions is an enormous opportunity to expand access to DHA’s services without the need for costly and time-consuming construction projects. He suggested that people will increasingly access government services, particularly those offered by DHA, through digital platforms, like the banking apps and bank branches, before physical buildings are completed.
He said that it was not possible to be more specific about the ETA launch date. There are very few departments in any government in the world that are working at the speed and urgency that DHA is working. The fact that DHA had lined up the ETA to launch before the end of September is a remarkable testament to the commitment of the Director-General (DG) and his team to deliver on these undertakings. This is a complex, technologically sophisticated project built around artificial intelligence (AI). DHA is targeting the end of September for the launch of this project, but this will depend on potential bug fixes and rollout processes.
He agreed with Adv Nonkonyana about executive undertakings being critical. He pointed out that the executive undertakings are essentially reflected in the Annual Performance Plan (APP) that was tabled earlier today. The APP targets relate to some of the items discussed today, including the expansion to more bank branches for access to services, the launch of kiosks, and the implementation of the ETA system. The APP is a more focused tool for measuring and monitoring progress. DHA’s priorities are very clear and focused on what it is trying to achieve, most importantly, to address client problems and improve service delivery.
Mr Tommy Makhode, DG, DHA, referred to Adv Nonkonyana’s comment about the disparity in DHA services. He said that some of the DHA offices have more modern features due to a different procurement approach. He mentioned the Lusikisiki office in the Eastern Cape as an example, where DHA used KUKA Development Corporation to build the office. DHA is continuously engaging with DPWI to track progress on the stalled projects. DHA has also been working on expanding its service reach by opening offices within shopping malls, especially those that have undergone extensions.
Mr Mavuso referred to Ms Mananiso’s suggestion about the CDWs. He noted that DHA has a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with COGTA to collaborate on CDWs. For example, CDWs have assisted with birth registration campaigns in Gauteng and other provinces.
He said that besides the construction projects, DHA also relies on DPWI to find alternative office space when DHA facilities are deemed a health hazard by the Department of Labour.
He referred to the comment about the substandard home affairs services in rural areas. He said that this was attributed to inadequate infrastructure and a lack of investment in those areas. He explained that a satellite link could improve connectivity for DHA offices in rural areas.
Closing remarks
The Chairperson expressed gratitude to the Minister, Deputy Minister, DHA officials, and Members of the Select Committee for their contributions in ensuring that South Africans receive the services they deserve. He commended the consistent energy and resolve to do things better.
He said that the Select Committee recognised the work that the Minister and Deputy Minister are doing and will hold them to the highest standard. This is also an opportunity for Parliament to raise the bar on oversight and accountability.
The meeting was adjourned.
