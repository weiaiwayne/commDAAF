# False Positive Analysis: AI Mentions in Non-AI Hearings

**Total False Positives**: 363 hearings (65% of search results)
**Analyzed Sample**: 50 hearings

---

## Context Categories

AI is mentioned in non-AI-focused hearings in these contexts:

### 1. List of Emerging Technologies (Most Common)
AI mentioned alongside other technologies as part of a list, not as the main topic.

**Examples**:
- "technologies like 5g, ai [artificial intelligence], and distributed cloud"
- "new technologies like artificial intelligence" (Music Modernization Act)
- "critical technologies like artificial intelligence" (CIRCIA cybersecurity)

### 2. Workforce/Automation Concerns
AI mentioned in context of job impacts or worker adaptation.

**Examples**:
- "older workers are encountering artificial intelligence" (Protecting Older Americans)
- "artificial intelligence (ai). quite the debate these days" (Workforce Dynamics)
- "the right people, like people with ai skills" (GAO workforce)

### 3. Government Modernization
AI mentioned as tool for improving government operations.

**Examples**:
- "artificial intelligence to identify, accelerate, and streamline" (IT Modernization)
- "artificial intelligence and its potential to help members" (GAO Modernization)
- "cloud-based ehr infused with artificial intelligence" (VA EHR)

### 4. National Security/Defense (Passing Mention)
AI mentioned briefly in national security context but not main topic.

**Examples**:
- "department of defense (dod), including artificial intelligence" (GAO Report)
- "for military solutions. in artificial intelligence, data" (Defense Travel)
- "responsibly deploying artificial intelligence technology" (DHS Appropriations)

### 5. China Competition (Passing Mention)
AI mentioned in China context but hearing is broader.

**Examples**:
- "sophisticated techniques, such as artificial intelligence" (Financial Aggression)
- "how ai tools can" (Countering China's Influence)

### 6. Specific Application Mentions
AI mentioned as tool for specific non-AI task.

**Examples**:
- "artificial intelligence can help search for and identify" (Missing Children)
- "artificial intelligence to generate" (Child Exploitation)
- "recognition and potentially artificial intelligence" (Aviation Security)

### 7. Budget/Appropriations Line Items
AI mentioned in funding context.

**Examples**:
- "artificial intelligence risk reduction, fairr act" (CFPB Report)
- "amend the artificial intelligence training for the acquisition" (Markup)

## Key Insight

Most false positives occur because:
1. **AI is listed as one of many technologies** in hearings about broader topics
2. **AI is mentioned as a tool** for solving problems in other domains
3. **Budget hearings** mention AI funding as line items
4. **Witness bios** mention AI expertise even if hearing is about something else

---

## Filtering Recommendation

To identify hearings **primarily about AI**, use:
- AI term density ≥5 per 1000 words, OR
- 20+ strong AI terms (artificial intelligence, machine learning, etc.)

This yields 198 valid AI hearings (35% of search results).
