## RQ1: How do users linguistically construct "the enemy" in war vs protest discourse?

### Theoretical Framework

Drawing on van Dijk's (1998) ideological square and Wodak's (2001) discourse-historical approach, we analyze:
1. **Naming practices** — How the enemy is identified
2. **Attribution strategies** — What actions/traits are ascribed
3. **Dehumanization markers** — Metaphors that strip humanity
4. **Moral framing** — Appeals to universal ethical standards

---

### A. Ukraine War: Enemy Construction of Russia

#### 1. Naming Practices

| Pattern | Examples | Frequency |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **State-as-enemy** | "Russia," "#Russia" | High |
| **Leader personification** | "Putin," "#PutinWarCriminal" | High |
| **Official metonymy** | "Lavrov," "Kremlin" | Medium |
| **Collective noun** | "Russians," "Russian forces" | Medium |
| **Fantasy dehumanization** | "Orcs from Mordor" | Low but notable |

**Key finding:** Ukraine discourse oscillates between **state-level** ("Russia made war crimes") and **leader-focused** ("Putin is evil Sauron") framing. The collective Russian people are less frequently targeted directly.

#### 2. Exemplar Analysis

**Post:** *"This is how low Putin's #Russia has sunk. Rape, murder, execute, pillage and steal everything that's of any value. There is no such thing as rule of law for these Orcs from Mordor (Russia) #Putin is the evil Sauron. All nations need to unite to defeat this extensional threat"*

**Discourse markers:**
- **Litany structure** ("Rape, murder, execute, pillage and steal") — accumulation of atrocities creates overwhelming moral case
- **Fantasy metaphor** ("Orcs from Mordor," "evil Sauron") — borrows Tolkien's moral clarity; positions conflict as absolute good vs evil
- **Existential framing** ("extensional threat") — frames as civilizational, not territorial dispute
- **Call for unity** ("All nations need to unite") — constructs global in-group against Russian out-group

**Post:** *"#Lavrov is a notorious liar. He can't concede that #Russia made war crimes, mocks the victims, ignoring facts and international rules, agreements and conventions."*

**Discourse markers:**
- **Character assassination** ("notorious liar") — attacks credibility, not just policy
- **Victim-centered** ("mocks the victims") — focuses on harm to innocents
- **Legal framing** ("international rules, agreements and conventions") — appeals to universal standards, positions Russia outside civilization

#### 3. Attribution Strategies

| Attribution | Example | Function |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| War crimes | "#RussianWarCrimes #RussianLooters #RussianRapists" | Legal delegitimization |
| Genocide | "Russian warfare... meets all key characteristics of genocide" | Maximal moral condemnation |
| Global harm | "Russia blockading... will lead to genocide in Africa" | Expands victimhood beyond Ukraine |

#### 4. Dehumanization Markers

The **"Orcs" metaphor** is particularly notable:
- Draws on globally-recognized narrative (Lord of the Rings)
- Implies mindless evil, lack of individual agency
- Creates moral permission for violence ("defeating orcs")
- Rare but appears in most emotionally charged posts

**Absence noted:** Unlike historical propaganda, no widespread animal metaphors ("rats," "cockroaches"). Dehumanization is more **fantastical** than **biological**.

### B. #MahsaAmini Protest: Enemy Construction of Iranian Regime

#### 1. Naming Practices

| Pattern | Examples | Frequency |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **System/regime** | "جمهوری اسلامی" (Islamic Republic) | High |
| **Morality police** | "گشت_کشتار" (death patrol) | Medium |
| **Leadership mockery** | "گوساله" (calf = fool) re: officials | Medium |
| **Collective "they"** | "شما" (you [plural]) | High |
| **Second-person accusation** | "تف به شرفتون" (shame on you) | Medium |

**Key finding:** Protest discourse uses **second-person address** far more frequently. The enemy is spoken *to*, not just *about*. This reflects proximity — protesters are addressing their own government.

#### 2. Exemplar Analysis

**Post:** *"اخه الان این چی دستشه؟ چی آتیش زده؟ چرا میزنی بیشرف؟ شما خودتون زنو دختر ندارید؟ من یکی از امروز به اسلام شما کافرم"*

Translation: *"What does she have in her hand? What did she burn? Why are you beating [her], shameless one? Don't you yourselves have women and daughters? From today, I am a kafir [unbeliever] to your Islam."*

**Discourse markers:**
- **Rhetorical questions** — demand justification, position violence as irrational
- **Kinship appeal** ("Don't you have women and daughters?") — invokes shared humanity
- **Religious rejection** ("kafir to your Islam") — distinguishes "their Islam" from true faith
- **Direct address** ("بیشرف" = shameless one) — confrontational, face-to-face

**Post:** *"برای تک تک کلماتی که پیش چشم یک ملت به معنایشان تجاوز کردید: خدمت، مردم، آزادی، استقلال، قانون..."*

Translation: *"For every word you raped of its meaning before an entire nation: service, people, freedom, independence, law..."*

**Discourse markers:**
- **Semantic violence metaphor** ("تجاوز" = rape/violate) — accuses regime of corrupting language itself
- **Cumulative listing** — like Ukraine discourse, builds overwhelming case
- **Collective witness** ("پیش چشم یک ملت" = before a nation's eyes) — positions nation as unified victim

#### 3. Attribution Strategies

| Attribution | Example | Function |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| Hypocrisy | "Government is trying to cover up... How much more will you eat?" | Moral delegitimization |
| Gender oppression | "For not letting female swimmers compete due to hijab" | Specific grievance naming |
| Economic betrayal | "For youth who worked hard for minimal wage jobs" | Material grievance |
| Selling the nation | "برای مملکتی که تقسیم کردید بین روسیه و چین" (For the country you divided between Russia and China) | Sovereignty betrayal |

#### 4. Dehumanization Markers

Notably **restrained**:
- "گوساله" (calf/fool) — mockery rather than dehumanization
- Officials are portrayed as **corrupt and hypocritical**, not inhuman
- The enemy's humanity is preserved to enable **moral shaming**

**Key contrast:** Ukraine dehumanizes through **fantasy** (Orcs); Iran protests preserve humanity for **shame** (shameless ones).

### C. Cross-Context Comparison

| Dimension | Ukraine War | #MahsaAmini |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Primary enemy** | State + Leader (Russia/Putin) | System + Enforcers (regime/morality police) |
| **Address mode** | Third person (about them) | Second person (to them) |
| **Dehumanization** | Fantasy metaphor (Orcs) | Moral shaming (shameless) |
| **Moral framework** | International law | Human rights + gender justice |
| **Victim focus** | Ukrainian nation + global poor | Iranian women + youth |
| **Collective guilt** | Ambiguous (state vs people) | Clear (regime, not nation) |
| **Call to action** | International intervention | Internal revolution |

### Theoretical Implications

1. **Proximity shapes rhetoric:** Iranians speaking to their own government use intimate, accusatory second-person. Ukrainians speaking to international audience use legalistic third-person.

2. **Dehumanization serves different functions:**
   - War: Moral permission for violence (Orcs = killable)
   - Protest: Shame and delegitimization (hypocrites = expose-able)

3. **Language of rights differs:**
   - War: International humanitarian law, genocide conventions
   - Protest: Bodily autonomy, personal freedom, economic opportunity

## RQ5: How does ironic/sarcastic framing function in political resistance discourse?

### Theoretical Framework

Drawing on Hutcheon (1994) and Billig (2005), we analyze:
1. **Markers of irony** — How is non-literal meaning signaled?
2. **Targets of irony** — Who/what is mocked?
3. **Functions of irony** — What political work does it do?
4. **Detection challenges** — Why do AI models struggle with irony?

---

### A. Irony Detection: Where Claude and Kimi Diverge

#### Case 1: Russia-Iran "Worship"

**Post:** *"📸Photo of the Day📸 🇷🇺🇮🇷 #Russia worships its true master."*

| Model | Coding | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Claude | INFORMATIONAL | Photo post, neutral framing |
| Kimi | INJUSTICE | Sarcastic criticism of Russia-Iran relationship |

**Analysis:**
- **Irony markers:** "worships its true master" — inverts power hierarchy (Russia = superpower, but "worships" Iran)
- **Emoji framing:** "📸Photo of the Day" — mimics neutral news format, creates ironic contrast
- **Function:** Mocks Russia's diplomatic positioning; implies weakness/desperation

**Why Claude missed it:** "Photo of the Day" + hashtags mimics informational format. Without cultural context (Russia-Iran relationship), literal reading is possible.

#### Case 2: EU Candidate Status

**Post:** *"⚡️⚡️⚡️The European Parliament supports granting #Ukraine and Moldova the status of EU candidates..."*

| Model | Coding | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Claude | SOLIDARITY | International support celebration |
| Kimi | HOPE | Future-oriented aspirational |

**Analysis:** 
This is **not ironic** — models disagree on frame but both see positive valence. Shows that Claude-Kimi disagreement isn't always about irony.

#### Case 3: Scania Trucks Rebuild

**Post:** *"Today @ScaniaGroup opens a brand new facility near @Kyiv. Scania trucks will help #Ukraine rebuild what #Russia destroys. Nothing can break the resilience of Ukrainians."*

| Model | Coding | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Claude | HOPE | Future rebuilding, resilience |
| Kimi | SOLIDARITY | International corporate support |

**Analysis:**
Both legitimate readings. The phrase "rebuild what Russia destroys" could be:
- HOPE: Focus on rebuilding, future
- SOLIDARITY: Focus on international actor (Scania) helping

**Lesson:** Not all disagreement indicates coding error. Hybrid frames are inherent to complex political discourse.

### B. Iranian Irony: Regime Mockery

#### Case 1: Internet + Online Classes

**Post:** *"بیشعور تر از جمهوری اسلامی بازم خودش. کلاسای دانشگاهها رو میخواد آنلاین برگزار کنه. نت رو هم قطع کرده 😂😂😂"*

Translation: *"More idiotic than the Islamic Republic is... itself. It wants to hold university classes online. It also cut the internet. 😂😂😂"*

**Irony markers:**
- Self-referential paradox (online classes + no internet)
- Laughing emojis (😂) signal absurdist humor
- "بیشعور" (idiot) + superlative structure

**Function:** Exposes regime incompetence through logical contradiction. Humor builds solidarity among protesters.

#### Case 2: Cigarette Price Increase

**Post:** *"سیگارو گرون کردن :)))"*

Translation: *"They made cigarettes more expensive :)))"*

**Analysis:**
Without context, this seems trivial. In context of massive protests about fundamental rights:
- Ironic understatement (amid death and oppression, cigarette prices matter?)
- Possible dark humor about "smaller" grievances
- ":)))" signals non-serious tone

**Function:** Coping through absurdism; highlights regime's distorted priorities.

### C. Irony Typology

| Type | Ukraine Example | Iran Example | Function |
|------|-----------------|--------------|----------|
| **Power inversion** | "Russia worships its true master" | N/A | Mock enemy's claimed strength |
| **Self-contradiction** | N/A | Online classes + no internet | Expose incompetence |
| **Understatement** | N/A | Cigarette prices amid revolution | Absurdist coping |
| **Format mimicry** | "📸Photo of the Day" | N/A | Subvert neutral framing |
| **Kinship challenge** | N/A | "Don't you have daughters?" | Weaponize shared humanity |

---

### D. Why AI Models Struggle with Political Irony

1. **Format mimicry:** Ironic posts often adopt "neutral" formats (Photo of the Day, breaking news emojis) that trigger INFORMATIONAL classification.

2. **Cultural knowledge:** Understanding "Russia worships Iran" requires knowing Russian foreign policy self-image as great power.

3. **Emoji ambiguity:** 😂 can signal genuine humor OR bitter/sarcastic laughter.

4. **Language-specific markers:** Persian ":)))" differs from English irony markers.

### E. Codebook Recommendations

Based on this analysis, propose new irony detection rules:

**Rule 9.1 (Power Inversion Check):** If post describes powerful actor as weak, subordinate, or foolish, consider INJUSTICE frame despite neutral formatting.

**Rule 9.2 (Self-Contradiction Check):** If post describes logically contradictory policy/action, consider evaluative frame (INJUSTICE or CALL_TO_ACTION).

**Rule 9.3 (Emoji Context):** Laughing emojis (😂, :))) in political content often signal bitter irony, not genuine humor. Weight toward evaluative frames.

**Rule 9.4 (Understatement Detection):** Trivial complaints amid crisis contexts may signal ironic commentary on regime priorities.

## Conclusion: Two Modes of Discursive Resistance

### War Discourse (Ukraine)
- **Enemy:** Constructed as civilizational threat, external, quasi-mythical ("Orcs")
- **Audience:** International community; legalistic framing for intervention
- **Irony:** Rare; discourse is earnest, urgent, literal
- **Goal:** Material/military support; moral clarity

### Protest Discourse (#MahsaAmini)
- **Enemy:** Constructed as hypocritical, incompetent, shameful (their own government)
- **Audience:** Fellow citizens + regime itself (second-person address)
- **Irony:** Common; absurdist humor as solidarity-building
- **Goal:** Delegitimization; collective awakening; revolution

### Key Insight
**Distance shapes discourse.** War with an external enemy produces earnest, legalistic appeals to international norms. Protest against one's own government produces ironic, shame-based, intimate address. Both are effective within their contexts, but serve different political functions.

## Methodological Reflection

### What AI Coding Missed
1. Second-person vs third-person address (crucial for understanding audience)
2. Irony detection (especially format mimicry)
3. Cultural knowledge (Russia-Iran relations, Persian humor markers)
4. Kinship appeals as rhetorical strategy

### What AI Coding Caught
1. Frame distributions (INJUSTICE dominant in war, SOLIDARITY in protest)
2. Valence patterns (negative in war, positive in protest)
3. Arousal differences (medium in war, high in protest)

### Recommendation
**Hybrid approach:** Use AI coding for initial categorization + distribution mapping, then deep qualitative analysis on disagreement cases and frame exemplars.
