# ICA 2026 Conference: Emergent Research Themes
**Source:** 76th Annual ICA Conference Program (Cape Town, June 2026)  
**Conference Theme:** Communication and Inequalities in Context

---

## Core Conference Themes

### 1. AI Across Every Subfield
AI is not siloed—it's integrated into virtually every research area:

- **AI in instruction**: ChatGPT in classrooms, algorithm aversion, multimodal communication with autistic students
- **Alternative AI futures**: Chinese AI imaginaries beyond Silicon Valley, techno-religion, epistemological violence
- **AI + journalism**: Labor impacts, employment effects, automation of verification
- **Human-AI relationships**: Romantic relationships with AI chatbots, parasocial attachment frameworks, "loving AI, losing humans"
- **AI + mental health**: Chatbots for psychological well-being, support-seeking behavior
- **AI + climate**: Human-AI co-creation of climate fiction, VR for psychological distance
- **AI + politics**: Algorithmic inequality, democratic processes online
- **AI literacy**: Among older adults, young people, across cultures

**Key insight:** AI is no longer a "technology" topic—it's embedded in health, politics, relationships, education, journalism, organizational communication.

---

### 2. TikTok as Central Research Site

Multiple dedicated sessions on TikTok:

- **Political TikTok**: Populism (Milei), far-right in Germany, cross-national analysis of 27 European countries
- **TikTok to RedNote**: Platform migration as geopolitical resistance
- **Gendered body representation**: Norway vs. Romania comparison
- **TikTok refugees**: Chinese social media responses to platform bans
- **Algorithmic inequality on TikTok/Douyin**: "Hierarchy of legitimacy" through content moderation

**Key insight:** TikTok is now THE platform for studying political communication, youth culture, and algorithmic governance.

---

### 3. Global South & Decolonial Perspectives

Strong emphasis on non-Western contexts:

- **Preconferences**: "Beyond the Divide: Global South Agendas," "Digital Publics and the Global South," "African and Global Media Representations"
- **African media systems**: Toward an African model of media systems
- **Anti-Apartheid to Palestine**: Historical solidarities
- **Decolonizing the visual**: Community, memory, resistance
- **Francophone Africa**: Anti-French sentiment on social media
- **Chinese platforms**: WeChat, Douyin, Bilibili research
- **Latin America**: Media & Communication in Global Latinidades

**Key insight:** The field is actively de-centering Western perspectives. Global South contexts are no longer "applications" of Western theory—they're theory-generating.

---

### 4. Platform Migration & Governance

- **RedNote migration**: TikTok users moving to Chinese alternatives
- **WeChat governance**: State of exception, restricted communication
- **Platform colonialism**: Asymmetric amplification, infrastructural power
- **DSA enforcement**: European platform regulation
- **Content moderation**: Creates "hierarchies of legitimacy"
- **Platform trespassing**: Politics of infrastructural control

**Key insight:** Platform governance is now a major subfield, with emphasis on how moderation creates and reinforces inequalities.

---

### 5. Mental Health & Wellbeing

Especially focused on youth:

- **Adolescent social media use**: Longitudinal effects, stress accumulation over months
- **Instagram posts/stories**: Sentiment analysis and wellbeing
- **Depression stigma**: Douyin health communication
- **Mental health influencers**: Cross-cultural correlates
- **Metaphors that hurt and heal**: Stigma in mental illness coverage
- **AI chatbots for wellbeing**: Evidence from PLS-SEM studies

**Key insight:** Mental health is no longer a side topic—it's central to platform studies, health communication, and youth research.

---

### 6. Algorithmic Inequality & Justice

- **Algorithmic visibility**: Domestic violence on Douyin
- **Content moderation inequality**: LGBTQ+ users, gender identity
- **Echo chambers**: LLM agent-based simulations
- **Algorithmic gatekeeping**: Reverse image search as gatekeeper
- **Matthew Effect**: Algorithm curation and ideological diversity
- **Algorithmic news attitudes**: HPV knowledge miscalibration

**Key insight:** "Algorithmic inequality" is emerging as a distinct concept—algorithms don't just amplify, they create hierarchies.

---

### 7. Climate & Environmental Communication

- **Psychological distance**: VR experiments, local framing
- **Climate-skeptic engagement**: Anger-driven, longitudinal analysis
- **Environmental journalism**: Intersectional citizen reporting
- **Climate activism**: Multi-platform advocacy coalitions
- **Heatwave framing**: Multimodal issue frames
- **Climate fiction**: Human-AI co-creation

**Key insight:** Climate communication is maturing methodologically—more experiments, more longitudinal designs, more multimodal analysis.

---

### 8. Gender, Sexuality & Race

- **LGBTQ+ platform experiences**: DACH region, self-censorship
- **Gendered narratives**: Digital public spheres
- **Trans/gender-diverse applicants**: Framing effects on employability
- **Black women faculty**: Co-autoethnographic narratives
- **Racialized scapegoat narratives**: Media power vs. social structure
- **African gender and media**: Barriers, agency, resilience

**Key insight:** Intersectionality is increasingly operationalized—not just acknowledged, but measured and modeled.

---

### 9. Disability Communication

Entire preconference dedicated to disability:

- **Accessibility audits**: Communication conference implications
- **Sighted-blind collaboration**: Mobile games
- **Visually impaired media consumption**: "Light Cinema" project
- **Autism debate**: Mapping claims for recognition
- **Disability across visual media forms**
- **Neurodiversity and interpersonal communication**

**Key insight:** Disability is mainstreaming in communication research—no longer a niche but a lens applied across contexts.

---

### 10. Journalism Under Pressure

- **Occupational intimidation**: Preconference on researcher protection
- **Journalistic lawfare**: Defamation lawsuits
- **Journalism in authoritarian contexts**: Variations of loyal-facilitator role
- **Greek Watergate**: Press freedom and surveillance tech
- **Generative AI + journalism**: Labor impacts, employment effects
- **Podcast journalism**: Building authority through conversation

**Key insight:** Journalism studies is increasingly focused on threats—legal, authoritarian, technological, economic.

---

### 11. Influencer Studies Evolution

- **Virtual influencers**: Longitudinal effects on parasocial relationships
- **Influencer professionalization**: Tension with authenticity
- **Mental health influencers**: Cross-cultural correlates
- **David Hogg ego network**: Digital influencer activism
- **Influencer as trader**: Legal/ethical boundaries
- **Gen Alpha influencers**: Emerging research area

**Key insight:** Influencer research is moving beyond "do they work?" to "how do parasocial relationships form, evolve, and break?"

---

### 12. Trust & Misinformation

- **Practicing trust**: Media, machines, methods
- **Science communication + trust**: Revisiting the issue
- **Consensus science communication**: Survey-experiment on collateral damage
- **Fake news literacy → political participation**: Mediation through media locus of control
- **Misinformation in short videos**: Mood-emotion-content nexus
- **AI-generated disinformation**: How audiences make sense of it

**Key insight:** Trust is no longer binary—it's processual, contextual, and requires longitudinal study.

## Methodological Trends at ICA 2026

1. **Causal Inference Institute**: Dedicated preconference on causal methods
2. **Computational Methods Hackathon**: Opening computational communication
3. **LLM agent-based simulations**: For studying echo chambers
4. **Longitudinal designs**: Multiple papers tracking effects over months
5. **Cross-national comparisons**: 27-country studies
6. **VR experiments**: For psychological distance research
7. **Network intervention analysis**: New method introduced
8. **Latent profile analysis**: For heterogeneity in depression symptoms

## Key Takeaways for AgentAcademy

1. **AI is everywhere**: Any study that ignores AI's role is already dated
2. **TikTok is central**: If studying youth/politics/algorithms, TikTok is required
3. **Global South matters**: Western-only samples are increasingly criticized
4. **Algorithmic inequality is a framework**: Not just "algorithms amplify"—they create hierarchies
5. **Mental health + platforms**: Major research growth area
6. **Causal methods expected**: Correlational studies face skepticism
7. **Longitudinal > cross-sectional**: Effects accumulate over time
8. **Intersectionality operationalized**: Not just acknowledged, measured
9. **Researcher vulnerability**: The field is reflecting on its own working conditions
10. **Trust as process**: Not a variable—a trajectory
