## What Makes a Study Interesting Right Now

### High-Value Research Questions (2026)

1. **Causal, not correlational**: The field is moving past "X correlates with Y" → demand experiments or quasi-experiments. If we can't do RCTs, need strong causal identification.

2. **Platform-specific mechanisms**: Generic "social media" studies are dated. Need to understand *how* TikTok's algorithm differs from X's, how affordances shape behavior differently.

3. **AI as subject AND method**: Studies that use AI methods to study AI phenomena are novel (e.g., using LLMs to analyze how AI-generated misinfo spreads).

4. **Global South contexts**: ICA 2026 theme. Western-centric samples are a known limitation—cross-cultural or non-Western contexts add value.

5. **Algorithmic amplification as mechanism**: Not just "what content goes viral" but *why* the algorithm promotes it—engagement optimization as causal driver.

## Theoretical Frameworks to Draw On

### For Framing Studies
- Gamson (1992) - injustice frames
- Entman (1993) - selection and salience
- Benford & Snow (2000) - collective action frames
- **New:** Computational framing (automated detection, multi-model validation)

### For Platform/Algorithmic Studies
- Affordance theory (boyd 2010, Bucher & Helmond 2018)
- Platform governance (Gillespie 2018, Gorwa 2019)
- Algorithmic curation (Napoli 2014, DeVito 2017)
- **New:** "Manufactured attention" typology (Rogers & Righetti 2025)

### For Coordination/Manipulation
- CIB detection (Giglietto et al. 2020)
- Bot detection (Varol et al. 2017, Botometer)
- **New:** Cross-platform coordination (Cinus et al. 2025)
- **New:** Attribution models (Thiele et al. 2025)

### For AI-Mediated Communication
- Human-machine communication (Guzman & Lewis 2020)
- Algorithmic gatekeeping (Thorson & Wells 2016)
- **New:** LLM-as-annotator validity debates (Abdurahman et al. 2025)

## Methodological Standards (2026)

### What Reviewers Expect

1. **Multi-model validation is becoming standard** for LLM-based coding
   - Single model = convenience sample of one
   - 3+ models = epistemic diversity
   - Report disagreements, don't just average

2. **Effect sizes, not just significance**
   - Cohen's d, η², IRR with benchmarks
   - "Significant" is not enough—how big?

3. **Baseline validation before anomaly claims**
   - We learned this from CLBD: establish what's "normal" first
   - Stratify by user type, platform, time period

4. **Reproducibility**
   - Share prompts verbatim
   - Report model versions, temperatures
   - Open-source models preferred for weight sharing

5. **Acknowledge algorithmic confounds**
   - Engagement metrics shaped by recommendation systems
   - Can't study "virality" without platform context

## What NOT to Do (Field Blind Spots We Can Avoid)

1. **Don't assume LLM agreement = validity**
   - Three models agreeing might just be three models wrong the same way
   - Need human validation for novel constructs

2. **Don't treat platforms as equivalent**
   - X ≠ TikTok ≠ Facebook
   - Affordances matter

3. **Don't ignore synthetic content**
   - Growing % of social media is AI-generated
   - May need detection as preprocessing

4. **Don't study coordination on only one side**
   - Our Belarus/Xinjiang lesson: both sides coordinate
   - Always check both

5. **Don't oversell exploratory findings**
   - Declare validation tier upfront
   - Exploratory ≠ publication-ready

## Gaps We Can Fill (Novel Contribution Opportunities)

### Methodological Gaps
- **Video/multimodal CIB detection**: Most methods are text-based; TikTok understudied
- **LLM reliability on subtle constructs**: Sarcasm, irony, implicit frames
- **Multi-model disagreement analysis**: What does systematic divergence tell us?

### Theoretical Gaps
- **Proximity and enemy construction**: Our work on Ukraine vs. Iran
- **Cross-layer network behavior**: CLBD negative finding needs follow-up—what DOES work?
- **Algorithmic amplification × framing**: How do recommendation systems interact with frame prevalence?

### Empirical Gaps
- **Non-Western platform data**: Weibo, VK, Telegram, regional platforms
- **Longitudinal algorithm effects**: Most studies are snapshots
- **AI-generated content in political discourse**: How much? What effect?

## When Designing an AgentAcademy Study, Ask:

1. **Is this question timely?** Does it address a 2025-2026 concern?
2. **What's the theoretical hook?** Which framework grounds it?
3. **Can we add causal leverage?** Natural experiment, quasi-experiment, before/after?
4. **Are we avoiding known pitfalls?** Platform conflation, single-model, no baseline?
5. **What's the novel contribution?** Method, theory, or empirical finding?
6. **Would this pass ICA/NCA review?** What would Reviewer 2 say?
