## The Communication Field Right Now

### Major Subfields and Their Hot Topics

#### 1. Health Communication
- **Vaccine hesitancy & prebunking**: Inoculation theory applied to misinfo—exposing weakened doses of misinfo before real exposure
- **Mental health + social media**: Inconsistent findings; peer relationships matter more than screen time; quality vs. quantity of use
- **AI in health messaging**: Virtual influencers correcting health misinfo as effectively as humans
- **Pregnant women & vaccine decisions**: Vulnerable population + social media correction strategies

#### 2. Science Communication  
- **Trust crisis**: Misinformation + polarization eroding public trust in expertise
- **Storytelling > data dumps**: "Dry data analysis is out, storytelling is in" (StatNews, 2025)
- **Science influencers**: Scientists with millions of followers (Dr. Noc, etc.) reshaping how public engages with science
- **Headline-only reading**: People trust headlines without reading further, amplifying misinfo

#### 3. Environmental Communication
- **Climate obstruction**: Fossil fuel industry mimics grassroots strategies; "subsidized publics" give appearance of organic support
- **Solutions journalism**: Shift from problem-focused to solution-focused climate coverage
- **Gen AI + climate**: AI both generates climate misinfo and helps combat it
- **Multi-platform activism**: #Lützerath protests as case study of advocacy coalitions across platforms

#### 4. Children, Adolescents & Media
- **Youth mental health crisis**: Unprecedented increases in anxiety, depression, body dysmorphia
- **Social media as correlate vs. cause**: Findings inconsistent; peer relationships stronger predictor than social media quality
- **Adolescents with mental health conditions**: Spend more time on social media, less happy about online friends
- **Content characteristics matter**: Not just time spent—what type of content (active vs. passive, positive vs. negative)

#### 5. Interpersonal & Relational Communication
- **AI in relationships**: Human-AI romantic relationships emerging ("Forever and Always, My Love" study)
- **AI reply suggestions**: Affect feelings of connectedness and relationship maintenance
- **Mediated interpersonal communication**: Major hiring area (UC Davis, 2026)
- **Technology-based adolescent relationships**: How digital communication shapes relationship competencies

#### 6. Creator Economy & Influencer Studies
- **Parasocial relationships**: Central to influencer effectiveness; authenticity paradox (influencers deemed inauthentic but still persuasive)
- **Virtual vs. human influencers**: AI influencers approaching human effectiveness; concerns about transparency
- **Gen Alpha influencers**: Oldest turn 16 in 2026; peer influence + parasocial especially strong
- **Commercialization fatigue**: Too many sponsored posts erodes trust

#### 7. Organizational Communication
- **Hybrid work as permanent**: 83% feel more productive remote/hybrid; asynchronous practices key
- **AI in workplace**: Microsoft's "Responsible AI Organizational Maturity Model"
- **Meeting overload**: Reducing synchronous requirements
- **Remote culture gap**: Only 21% believe their org has strong remote/hybrid culture

#### 8. Political Communication
- **Algorithmic polarization**: Science paper (Nov 2025) shows feed ranking *causally* affects polarization
- **Platform-independent auditing**: Browser extensions let researchers study algorithms without platform cooperation
- **Encrypted messaging**: Preconference theme at ICA 2026—platforms, accountability, hate speech
- **Global South perspectives**: ICA 2026 theme = "Communication and Inequalities in Context"

#### 9. Journalism Studies
- **AI existential threat**: Answer engines cut referral traffic 43%; "squeeze" from AI + creator economy
- **GenAI in newsrooms**: Automated story treatment, headlines, fact-checking
- **Video podcast dominance**: Competing with traditional video; Deloitte 2026 predictions
- **Surveillance of journalists**: Greek Watergate scandal—press freedom + surveillance tech

#### 10. Privacy & Surveillance
- **EU AI Act full implementation**: August 2026; prohibits harmful manipulation, untargeted facial recognition
- **DSA data access**: Article 40 enabling researcher access to platform internal data
- **Children's online privacy**: ICO investigating mobile games
- **State privacy law proliferation**: Multiple US states enforcing new laws

#### 11. Platform Studies
- **TikTok as distinct ecosystem**: Video-first CIB detection understudied; different affordance structure
- **X/Twitter transformation**: Post-Musk changes; Grok controversies; enforcement actions
- **Platform governance maturation**: DSA two-year mark; enforcement patterns emerging
- **Cross-platform coordination**: Tracking manipulation across X, TikTok, Facebook, YouTube

#### 12. Audio/Podcast Research
- **70% of Americans have listened to a podcast** (Edison 2025)
- **Social podcasting**: Evolution from passive consumption to active participation
- **Video podcasts**: Competing with traditional video; hybrid format growing
- **Deep engagement**: 6.3 hours/week among weekly listeners

## Methodological Currents

### What's Gaining Ground
1. **Multi-model LLM validation** — Using 3+ models for reliability; reporting disagreements
2. **Causal identification** — Moving beyond correlation; experiments, quasi-experiments, IV designs
3. **Platform-independent auditing** — Browser extensions to study algorithms without API access
4. **Longitudinal designs** — Tracking change over time, not just snapshots
5. **Mixed methods** — Computational + qualitative in same study
6. **Pre-registration** — Committing to analysis plan before data collection
7. **Effect sizes** — Not just significance; Cohen's d, practical significance

### What's Being Criticized
1. **Single-platform studies** — X ≠ TikTok ≠ Facebook; affordances matter
2. **WEIRD samples** — Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic overrepresentation
3. **Cross-sectional designs** — Can't establish causation or temporal order
4. **Engagement as outcome** — Shaped by algorithms; confounded by platform design
5. **LLM agreement as validity** — Models agreeing doesn't mean they're right
6. **p-hacking** — Multiple comparisons without correction; selective reporting

## Theoretical Frameworks in Use

### Classic Foundations
- **Framing** (Entman 1993; Gamson 1992)
- **Agenda-setting** (McCombs & Shaw 1972)
- **Uses and gratifications** (Katz, Blumler, Gurevitch 1974)
- **Social identity theory** (Tajfel & Turner 1979)
- **Parasocial interaction** (Horton & Wohl 1956)

### Contemporary Extensions
- **Affordance theory** (boyd 2010; Bucher & Helmond 2018)
- **Networked publics** (boyd 2010)
- **Algorithmic gatekeeping** (Thorson & Wells 2016)
- **Human-machine communication** (Guzman & Lewis 2020)
- **Platform governance** (Gillespie 2018; Gorwa 2019)

### Emerging Frameworks
- **Manufactured attention** (Rogers & Righetti 2025) — Typology of CIB
- **Inoculation 2.0** — Prebunking applied to digital misinfo
- **Proximity and enemy construction** — How distance shapes rhetoric (our work)
- **Algorithmic amplification as mechanism** — Not just what spreads, but why algorithms promote it

## When Designing Any Study, Ask:

1. **Which subfield(s) does this speak to?** Health? Political? Interpersonal? Organizational?
2. **What theoretical framework grounds it?** Not ad-hoc—what existing theory does it test/extend?
3. **Is this WEIRD-centric?** Could it be replicated in non-Western contexts?
4. **Platform-specific or generalizable?** Am I treating platforms as equivalent when they're not?
5. **Cross-sectional or longitudinal?** Can I make causal claims with this design?
6. **Who cares outside academia?** Policy implications? Industry relevance? Public benefit?
7. **What would the best journal for this be?** JOC? NM&S? Health Communication? Political Communication?

## Quick Intuition Checks

| If studying... | Ask yourself... |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Social media effects | "Effects on what? Mediated by what? For whom?" |
| Misinformation | "Detection, spread, or correction? Which platform? What topic domain?" |
| Algorithms | "Can I establish causation? Or just correlation with engagement?" |
| Youth/adolescents | "Am I conflating screen time with content type?" |
| Political discourse | "Am I checking both sides for coordination/manipulation?" |
| AI/LLMs | "Am I contributing to validity or just reliability?" |
| Health | "What's the intervention implication?" |
| Influencers | "Parasocial relationship as mechanism or outcome?" |
