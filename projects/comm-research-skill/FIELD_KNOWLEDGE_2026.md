## Emergent Research Topics (2025-2026)

### 1. 🤖 AI/LLM in Communication Research

**Key developments:**
- **LLMs as annotators**: Growing body of work comparing LLM vs. human coding (Bojić et al. 2025 found comparable reliability for sentiment/political leaning, but both struggle with sarcasm)
- **Validation frameworks**: New primers for evaluating LLMs in social science (Abdurahman et al. 2025 in Advances in Methods and Practices in Psychological Science)
- **Open-source emphasis**: DeepSeek, Mistral, LLaMA preferred for reproducibility—can share model weights like codebooks
- **Epistemic concerns**: Debate over whether LLM agreement = reliability or just "consistent hallucination"

**Key papers:**
- "Comparing LLMs and human annotators in latent content analysis" (Scientific Reports, July 2025)
- "A Primer for Evaluating LLMs in Social-Science Research" (SAGE, 2025)
- "The use of LLMs to annotate data in management research" (Strategic Management Journal, Oct 2025)

**CommDAAF implication:** Our multi-model approach is aligned with field direction—different models as epistemic diversity.

**Key papers:**

**CommDAAF implication:** News framing studies need to account for AI-generated content as both subject and methodological tool.

### 3. 🎭 Coordinated Inauthentic Behavior (CIB)

**Key developments:**
- **Cross-platform detection**: New methods for tracking coordination across X, TikTok, Facebook (Cinus et al. 2025)
- **TikTok CIB**: Video-first platforms present unique detection challenges (arXiv, Oct 2025)
- **AI-driven manipulation**: NATO StratCom 2026 report on synthetic influence operations
- **Typology development**: Rogers & Righetti (2025) propose typology of "manufactured attention"
- **Attribution challenges**: Thiele et al. (2025) theoretical model for attributing CIB to actors

**Key papers:**
- "Coordinated inauthentic behaviour on Facebook? A typology of manufactured attention" (SAGE, 2025)
- "Exposing Cross-Platform Coordinated Inauthentic Activity in the Run-Up to the 2024 U.S. Election" (ACM WWW, 2025)
- "Coordinated Inauthentic Behavior on TikTok" (arXiv, Oct 2025)

**CommDAAF implication:** Our Coordination Detector skill is well-positioned; should add TikTok/video detection methods.

### 4. ⚖️ Platform Governance & DSA

**CommDAAF implication:** DSA data access creates new research opportunities—build skills for VLOP data analysis.

### 5. 📊 Algorithmic Amplification & Polarization

**Key developments:**
- **Causal evidence**: Science paper (Nov 2025) shows feed reranking causally affects affective polarization by 2+ points
- **Algorithm auditing without platform cooperation**: New methods using browser extensions to study feeds independently
- **Engagement vs. satisfaction**: Engagement-based ranking amplifies divisive content (PMC, 2025)
- **Echo-bubble effects**: Chiridza & Mare (2025) demonstrate how personalization amplifies polarization + misinformation simultaneously

**Key papers:**
- "Reranking partisan animosity in algorithmic social media feeds alters affective polarization" (Science, Nov 2025)
- "Designing social media content recommendation algorithms for societal good" (Annals of NY Academy of Sciences, May 2025)
- "Algorithmic influence and media legitimacy" (Frontiers in Communication, Sep 2025)

**CommDAAF implication:** Framing studies should account for algorithmic amplification as confound; need platform context.

## Methodological Trends

1. **Multi-model validation**: Using multiple LLMs for reliability (aligned with CommDAAF)
2. **Platform-independent auditing**: Browser extensions to study feeds without API access
3. **Cross-platform analysis**: Tracking phenomena across X, TikTok, Facebook, YouTube
4. **Causal inference**: Moving beyond correlational studies (randomized feed experiments)
5. **Computational + qualitative**: Mixed methods combining automated analysis with close reading
6. **Open science emphasis**: Reproducibility, pre-registration, open-source tools

---

## Gaps & Opportunities

1. **TikTok/video-first CIB detection**: Mostly text-based methods; video understudied
2. **Global South perspectives**: Most research Western-centric; ICA 2026 theme addresses this
3. **LLM validity (not just reliability)**: Inter-model agreement ≠ ground truth
4. **Longitudinal algorithm effects**: Most studies cross-sectional
5. **Multimodal content analysis**: Images, video, audio integration with text
