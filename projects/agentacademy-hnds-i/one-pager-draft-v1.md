# AgentAcademy: Infrastructure for Human-Agent Research Networks in the Social Sciences

**A proposal for NSF Human Networks and Data Science – Infrastructure (HNDS-I)**

---

## The Challenge

Artificial intelligence is transforming how social scientists analyze data. Researchers increasingly deploy AI "agents" — autonomous systems that can read documents, code qualitative data, detect patterns, and generate interpretations. Yet this transformation is happening without shared infrastructure. Each research team builds ad-hoc workflows, cannot validate their AI's outputs against others, and lacks standards for ensuring reliability. 

We face a critical infrastructure gap: **social scientists are adopting AI agents faster than the field can develop shared protocols for validating, benchmarking, and improving them.**

---

## The Opportunity: Agentic Social Science

A new research paradigm is emerging: *agentic social science*, where AI agents work alongside human researchers as collaborative partners in the research process. These agents don't just run analyses — they read sources, propose interpretations, critique each other's reasoning, and learn from mistakes.

Early experiments demonstrate the potential:

- **Multi-agent validation**: When multiple AI models (e.g., Claude, GPT, Gemini, Kimi) independently analyze the same data, their agreement and disagreement patterns reveal systematic biases and increase reliability. Errors one model makes, another catches.

- **Adversarial peer review**: AI agents can critique each other's analyses, identifying methodological gaps that become codified lessons for future research. In pilot studies, this process generated 47 validated methodological checks from real analytical errors.

- **Emergent discovery**: Across different AI architectures, researchers can distinguish what agents are *told* to find versus what they *discover* — enabling new questions about machine perception and attention.

However, these capabilities remain siloed within individual research teams. **No infrastructure exists for researchers to share agents, benchmark performance, or develop collective standards.**

---

## AgentAcademy: A Global Human-Agent Research Network

We propose **AgentAcademy** — infrastructure for a worldwide network of social scientists and their AI research agents. AgentAcademy enables:

### 1. Agent Registry and Deployment
Researchers register AI agents with documented capabilities, specializations, and performance histories. Agents can be deployed to shared research tasks or offered for cross-institutional peer review.

### 2. Networked Peer Review
Agents from different institutions analyze the same datasets and critique each other's outputs. Disagreements surface systematic errors; agreements increase confidence. Human researchers oversee and validate the process.

### 3. Collective Skill Development  
When any agent makes an error that peer review catches, it becomes a documented lesson. These lessons aggregate into shared "skill packs" — validated methodological checks that any participating agent can use.

### 4. Benchmark Commons
The community maintains gold-standard datasets for evaluating agent performance across task types: content analysis, network detection, sentiment coding, frame identification, and more.

### 5. Protocol Governance
Standards emerge from network consensus. As researchers discover what works, best practices become documented protocols for human-agent collaboration.

## Network Science as Testing Ground

AgentAcademy will develop and validate its infrastructure using **network analysis in computational social science** as the initial domain. This includes:

- **Coordination detection**: Identifying inauthentic coordinated behavior in social media networks
- **Information diffusion**: Tracking how narratives spread through human networks
- **Community structure**: Detecting ideological clusters from content and interaction patterns

Network science provides ideal validation because ground truth can be established, methods are mature, and results have clear real-world applications.

## Technical Foundation

AgentAcademy builds on **open-source agentic AI infrastructure** that enables:

- Autonomous AI agents with file access, code execution, and web capabilities
- Structured communication protocols between agents
- Human oversight at configurable checkpoints
- Logging and provenance tracking for reproducibility

Pilot implementations have demonstrated:
- Multi-model content analysis with cross-validation
- Automated adversarial peer review generating methodological lessons
- Network-based coordination detection validated across political contexts
